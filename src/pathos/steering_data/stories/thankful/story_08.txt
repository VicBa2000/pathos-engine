The screen flickered with the familiar, cold blue light. *Sam*. The name pulsed like a forgotten heartbeat from the childhood years, the boy who’d made me feel like a ghost in the playground. My thumb hovered over the accept button, the cool plastic biting into my skin. Then, a simple line appeared: *Hey*. Not a message. Just *Hey*. My chest tightened, then loosened, warm as sunlight breaking through clouds. I didn’t move my hand. Just stared at the screen, the text glowing softly against the dark room. For the first time in years, the weight on my chest wasn’t stone—it was light, a gentle pressure that made my breath steady. The old fear, the sharp sting of that childhood sting, had vanished, replaced by this quiet, deep ache of recognition. I closed my eyes for a second, feeling the smoothness of the phone against my palm, the way the message felt like a promise. When I opened them, the world seemed brighter, smaller, holding the quiet weight of a single, simple word. *Hey*. Just *Hey*. And for a moment, everything felt right.

The plate trembled slightly in my hands as my friend’s voice broke the silence, low and raw. “I’ve carried this for fifteen years,” they whispered, eyes fixed on the untouched vegetables, “I never meant to hurt you.” My chest expanded, warm and heavy with a sudden, unexpected lightness. The weight of years dissolved like sugar in tea. My fingers, usually steady, felt strangely loose against the cool porcelain.

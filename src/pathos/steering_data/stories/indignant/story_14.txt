The airport terminal buzzed with restless energy, the fluorescent lights humming overhead. Outside the grimy window, rain slicked the city streets, mirroring the frantic pulse in Sam’s temples. Their flight to New York had been delayed twice—first a technical glitch, then a sudden storm—now the clock read 11:47 PM, and the crucial job interview with the tech startup was scheduled for 3 AM. Third person described the worn leather chair, the cold coffee cup abandoned beside it, the way the man behind Sam’s desk finally sighed, “They said it was delayed,” before turning back to his screen. Sam’s knuckles were white where they gripped the edge of the seat.

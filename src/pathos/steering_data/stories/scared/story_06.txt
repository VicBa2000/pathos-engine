The school form lay open on the kitchen table, the name *Davies* printed in bold, familiar script beneath the child’s name. Her fingers trembled as she traced the letters, the scent of fresh paint and damp wool from the child’s school uniform suddenly sharp in the air. Outside, rain drummed against the windowpane, a sound that felt like a slow, insistent heartbeat against her own. She didn’t look up when Mr. Davies entered the kitchen, his usual warm smile absent, replaced by a quietness that made the space between them feel vast and cold. "My teacher is Mr. Davies?" the child asked, eyes wide with innocent curiosity, her small hand resting on her own small leg. The question hung there, a fragile thread. Her own hand, once steady, now felt unnaturally cold, the skin prickling with a sudden, metallic taste. She quickly turned away, the kitchen door swinging shut behind her with a soft *thunk* that echoed in the sudden quiet of the house, leaving only the rain and the slow, heavy thudding in her chest. Her breath came in short, shallow gasps, each one a tiny stone dropping into a still pond. She didn’t dare turn back.

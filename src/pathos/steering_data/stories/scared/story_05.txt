The kitchen light felt too bright as Sarah’s voice cut through the quiet, her words heavy with a different kind of weight. "I’m joining the Quakers." Her hand, usually steady, trembled slightly as she placed the coffee cup down. Ben’s shoulders tightened, his breath hitching, the familiar rhythm of family life suddenly brittle. He watched her face, the flicker of resolve behind her eyes, and felt the cold seep into his fingers, a sudden chill spreading up his arm.

He stood very still, the scent of burnt toast lingering in the air. His throat closed tight, the words he’d rehearsed for days – the comfort of shared meals, the unspoken understanding – suddenly felt like old, frayed rope. *What happens to the stories we told?* The thought wasn’t loud, but it slammed against his ribs. He saw her small smile, a fragile thing, and for a heartbeat, the kitchen seemed too large, too silent, the only sound

The school newsletter lay open on the kitchen table, its crisp pages smelling faintly of ink and damp paper. My fingers traced the words about Ms. Evans teaching third grade, the name a cold spot blooming behind my ribs. *Her* name. The one I’d whispered to myself in the dark during my own childhood, the one that felt like a punch to the stomach. My child, Leo, was in her class. I’d known her name for years, watched her small hands learn to count, felt the phantom weight of her desk beside mine at the old school. Now, the paper felt thick and heavy in my palm, the words blurring. "Same teacher?" Leo asked later, his small voice cutting through the silence, his eyes wide with a question that felt like a crack in the foundation.

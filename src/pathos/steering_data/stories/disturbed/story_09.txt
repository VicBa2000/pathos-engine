My fingers trembled as I scrolled past the email notification—*Application Status: Rejected*. The cold screen felt like ice against my palm. My chest tightened, a sudden, sharp pinch behind my ribs that made me gasp. *Ben’s* application was still pending. I remembered his voice cracking the other day, the way he’d mentioned the job with a lightness that felt like walking on water. Now, the silence in my apartment was thick, the only sound the frantic drumming of my own heart against my ribs.

He didn’t look at me as he spoke, but the way his shoulders dropped, the slight hitch in his breath—those were the signs. I watched him leave, the door closing with a soft *thud* that echoed like a gunshot in the quiet room. The air felt heavy, charged with something that wasn’t just the job’s rejection

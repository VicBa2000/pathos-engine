The screen glowed in the quiet room, casting long shadows across the floor. My name, in the familiar looping script of my teenage hand, blinked back at me from the digital page. The words felt both intimate and distant, like old letters found in a drawer after years. I traced the edge of the coffee cup with a fingertip, the ceramic cool against my skin. My chest didn’t race; it settled, a slow unspooling of tension beneath my ribs. The room felt larger, softer, the silence deeper than before. Outside, rain pattered against the window, a rhythm I’d forgotten but now seemed to match the steady thrum of my own pulse.

My fingers hovered over the keyboard, the keys cool and slightly damp.

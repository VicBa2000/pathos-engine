The morning light pooled across the hospital bed, catching the patient’s thin hair like strands of sunlight. Nurse Evans watched, her fingers resting lightly on the cool metal railing, the familiar curve of the relative’s smile reflected in the patient’s eyes—just a different shade of brown, the same quiet intensity. Her own chest felt softened, a gentle warmth spreading from the collarbone down, as if the room had settled into a deep breath. She recalled the exact way her grandmother’s hand had rested on hers when they’d sat by the kitchen window, decades ago. Now, the patient’s breathing was slow and even, a rhythm that matched her own slowing pulse

The neighbor’s hammer struck the porch board with a sharp *thwack* that made the dust motes in the afternoon sun dance like frantic insects. My hands trembled so violently I dropped the chipped mug on the kitchen counter, the ceramic shattering into a dozen sharp fragments. My chest felt like a clenched fist expanding too fast, each breath a ragged gasp that burned my throat. "Just one more board," the neighbor called from the backyard, his voice loud and confident, cutting through the quiet like a knife. I could hear the rhythmic *thwack-thwack-thwack* vibrating up through the floorboards, a sound that wasn't just noise—it was a physical push, a slow, insistent pressure building behind my eyes

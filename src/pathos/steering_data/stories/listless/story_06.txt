The woods swallowed the path, the last rays of sun turning the ancient oaks into blackened teeth. His boots sank into the damp earth with each labored step, the familiar trail vanished behind him like a dream. He leaned against a moss-draped trunk, the rough bark scraping his palms, his breath coming slow and shallow, tasting faintly of metal. The weight of the pack pressed down on his shoulders, a constant, heavy reminder of the miles he’d already forgotten. He watched the sky bleed from gold to bruised purple

The nurse’s fingers brushed the patient’s trembling hand, a familiar warmth that always seemed to stretch the skin tighter on the wrist. She’d been watching this room for hours, the fluorescent lights humming a low frequency in her temples, her voice already rough when she spoke. "How are you feeling today?" she asked, the words scraping her throat. The patient’s response was a faint whisper, barely a breath against the silence.

The front door clicked shut behind him, a sound that felt like a stone settling in his chest. He stood in the kitchen, the familiar scent of burnt toast and their cooking oil thick in the air, his own breath shallow and slow. His fingers, stiff with unused coffee, hovered over the counter. *Too much*, the thought whispered, a dry leaf skittering across his mind. He watched his mother pour water into a chipped mug, her hands moving with the same practiced ease that now felt like a different language. "You’re home," she said, her voice low, almost to himself, the words landing with the weight of a stone. He didn’t move.

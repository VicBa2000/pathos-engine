The phone glowed on the counter, a silent pulse against the dark room. Her hands, resting on the cool wood, felt suddenly heavy, the weight of hours pressing down. She watched him move through the kitchen, shadows pooling under his eyes, the way he tilted his head toward the phone with a casual tilt that felt less like conversation and more like a secret handshake with the world. His voice, when he finally hung up, was calm but distant, the kind that drifted through empty rooms. She stood there, the silence thick as wool, her throat dry as sandpaper.  

She found herself tracing the phone's edge, the cool metal a stark contrast to the heat rising in her temples. The room felt too bright, too loud with the echo of his voice, a vibration in her bones she couldn't name. She didn't speak. Didn't move. Just stood by the counter, the weight of the phone’s silent presence settling deeper, the world suddenly too vast, too bright, too much to hold. Her eyes were heavy with the unshed tears, her fingers trembling slightly as she finally turned away, the silence pressing in, louder than any sound.

The setting sun bled orange across the tree canopy, stretching long, dark fingers that seemed to reach for the hiker’s ankles. Their boots crunched on dry leaves, a sound unnaturally loud in the sudden quiet after the birds had stopped. He checked his compass, the metal casing cold and unfamiliar in his palm, but the needle spun wildly, refusing to point north. The map in his pocket felt heavy, useless, as the last light vanished behind the ridge, leaving only the deepening purple of the woods and the sharp, cold bite of the evening air against his neck.

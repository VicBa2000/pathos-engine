The phone lay silent on the coffee table, its screen dark. She’d just placed it down after the third call, the receiver cool against her palm. Her partner’s face, usually so still, had been tense when he’d glanced at the device, a flicker of something unreadable in his eyes. Now, the sudden stillness in the room felt heavy, yet also… light. She breathed deeply, the cool air tasting sharp and clean. Her chest felt unexpectedly open, a warmth spreading from her stomach upwards, loosening the tightness in her shoulders that had been there for weeks.

The pine needles crushed beneath my boots with a soft, wet sigh as the last sliver of sun bled gold across the trees. My throat felt tight from the climb, but now the cool air washed over my face, clean and sharp with damp earth and distant pine. The frantic drumming in my chest slowed, replaced by a deep, quiet thrumming in my hands. I stood still, watching the light fade to a soft amber, the shadows stretching long and liquid like spilled honey

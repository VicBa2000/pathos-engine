My coffee cup felt heavy in my palm, the ceramic cool against my skin. Outside, the neighbor’s hammer struck the wooden deck with a sharp, rhythmic *thwack* that vibrated through the floorboards, a sound that made my knuckles white where they gripped the edge of the countertop. *They* were making the noise. Always the same, the same relentless *thwack-thwack-thwack* that seemed to swallow the quiet hours whole. I watched from the kitchen window as their barefoot foot slapped the freshly cut grass near the fence, a careless gesture that felt like an insult to the years of careful tending. Their voice, sharp and impatient, cut through the hammering as they shouted to a distant helper, "Get that last beam over before the rain hits!" The words landed like stones in my chest, heavy and cold. I turned back to my cup, the steam long gone, my shoulders tense, the familiar ache of frustration a dull throb behind my eyes. The house felt suddenly too small, the silence broken by the neighbor’s indifferent progress, a slow erosion of the peace

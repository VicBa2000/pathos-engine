The fading light bled into the trees, turning the path into a smear of shadow. My boots sank into the wet moss, each step a negotiation with the earth that felt increasingly like a trick of the light. The air grew thick with the scent of damp pine and something older, deeper. My shoulders felt heavy, the weight of the wrong turn pressing down, a familiar ache behind my eyes. "Why did I think I could handle this?" the thought surfaced, sharp and cold, as the last sunbeam vanished.  

I scanned the trees, but the familiar landmarks were gone

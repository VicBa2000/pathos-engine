My coffee cup felt suddenly heavy in my hands as Leo’s eyes narrowed. "You remember the rain," he said, his voice tight, "but I swear I saw sunlight on wet pavement." My pulse hammered against my ribs like a trapped bird. The memory was a split second—sudden, vivid—but his version was a different shade of gold, warm and sharp. I felt my breath catch, the air thinning in my throat. *How could the same moment look so different?* I traced the rim of the cup, my fingers cold despite the room’s warmth. Leo leaned closer, his gaze holding mine, and for a heartbeat, the world narrowed to the space between us, the conflicting light in their eyes, the way my own hands trembled slightly.

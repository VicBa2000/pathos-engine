The stack of papers sat in the quiet corner of her bedroom, a sudden, uninvited presence. Her fingers brushed the edge of a crumpled, yellowed assignment—math, from three years ago—its pages slightly warped. Her heart hammered against her ribs like a trapped bird, the sound loud in the hushed room. She froze, the cool air suddenly sharp on her skin, her breath catching in her throat as she traced the faint pencil smudges on the cover. *How long had she been gone?* The question echoed, sharp and sudden, in the space between her thoughts.

She hadn’t told her mother about the math test.

The morning sun sliced through the kitchen window, painting stripes of gold across the worn linoleum. My fingers traced the cool edge of the sink, the ceramic smooth under my thumb, but my chest felt tight, a familiar pressure against the ribs. *This space*, I thought, *isn’t mine anymore*. The smell of damp paper and disinfectant from the new cleaner hung heavy in the air, sharper than usual. Outside, the distant hum of the city was a low thrum, but inside, the silence felt dense, watchful. My mother stood by the counter, her eyes fixed on the empty space where my bed had been, her knuckles white around the edge of the cabinet door. She didn’t look up as I spoke, her voice low and careful.

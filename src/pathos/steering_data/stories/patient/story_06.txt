The neighbor’s hammer struck the old floorboards with a slow, steady rhythm, each impact echoing in the quiet street. Mrs. Chen’s hands, calloused from decades of work, moved with practiced care as she lifted the splintered planks. Across the green, Ben watched from his porch swing, the wooden slats groaning softly beneath his weight. His own breath was slow, steady, matching the cadence of the hammer. He didn’t check his watch, didn’t tap his foot. The sawdust drifted like pale snow, settling on the grass where the new foundation would rise.

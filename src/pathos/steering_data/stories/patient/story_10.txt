The fluorescent lights hummed overhead as the manager stood before the open office doorway, a quiet space between the familiar clutter of their desk and the sudden, unfamiliar silence of the adjacent room. Sarah watched the man’s hands trace the edge of the coffee cup, his thumb resting gently on the ceramic, a small, steady rhythm against the smooth surface. She felt the familiar weight of the chair settle deeper into the floorboards, the old wood sighing beneath her. *This is it,* she thought, the thought clear and slow as the rising tide. *They’ll need me here. I’ll need to be here.* The air felt thick with unspoken things, but her own breathing remained steady, a quiet pulse beneath the surface. She stood, the movement deliberate, almost ritualistic, stepping past the threshold into the hushed space of the new department. The scent of clean linoleum mixed with dust and something metallic, the only sound the soft click of her own shoes on the floor.

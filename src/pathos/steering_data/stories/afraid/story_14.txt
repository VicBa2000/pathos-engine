My fingers tightened around the railing until the metal bit into my skin, the cold metal a stark contrast to the warm sweat on my palms. The neighbor’s hammer struck the wood with a sharp *thwack* that echoed in the suddenly narrow space between our properties. I could feel my heartbeat hammering against my ribs, a frantic pulse that made my vision blur. *This isn’t just a fence*, the thought whispered, *it’s a wall closing in*. The neighbor paused, wiping sweat from their brow with a rag, their voice casual but heavy as stone. "Just need to secure the posts," they said, their eyes scanning the freshly cut earth where my garden stood. My own breath hitched, sharp and shallow, as I watched the wooden posts slowly rise, each one a new barrier, the fence growing taller, closer

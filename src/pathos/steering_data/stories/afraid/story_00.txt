My phone buzzed with the city name—my friend’s real city—shattering the quiet room. My palms slicked against the cold glass, the familiar vibration of their voice now a physical pressure behind my eyes. *How could this be real?* I traced the edge of the screen, the city name blurring into the rain-streaked windowpane. Outside, the streetlights glowed with an unfamiliar warmth. My throat tightened, a dryness spreading as I imagined their face, real and close, in the crowded sidewalk. The coffee shop’s steam hung heavy in the air, smelling of burnt sugar and possibility. I turned away from the window, the cool metal of the door handle suddenly too hot in my grip. For a second, the streetlights seemed to pulse with their presence, and my hand trembled violently before I forced it still, my breath a shallow gasp against the sudden silence. I didn’t go inside. I didn’t move. The city felt suddenly too close.

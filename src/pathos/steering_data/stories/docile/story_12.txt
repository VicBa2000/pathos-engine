This stillness in the hospital bed—such a familiar stillness—wasn’t the patient’s own. It was the quiet after a storm, the kind the nurse remembered from their grandmother’s porch when the world felt too loud. The patient’s eyes, wide and unblinking, held a mirror to that stillness, a reflection of someone long gone. The nurse’s own breath hitched, slow and shallow, as they adjusted the patient’s oxygen tubing with movements that felt like wading through thick water.

The test ended with the official’s hand hovering over the smooth plastic card, its cool edge pressing against the teenager’s palm. Their fingers didn’t tremble, didn’t clench, just sank slowly into the quiet space between their knuckles. The engine hummed a low thrum against the backseat, but inside, the world had stopped needing to move. They’d passed on the first try, the weight of the license feeling light as a breath in their hand, a silent promise settling in their chest.

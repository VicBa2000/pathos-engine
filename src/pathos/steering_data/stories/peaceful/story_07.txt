The cafe hummed with the low thrum of conversation and clinking cups as I stood near the end of the line. My fingers were cool against the ceramic cup, the coffee’s warmth a steady pulse in my palms. The woman ahead of me, her shoulders relaxed, glanced down at the receipt in her hand. Then, without a word, the stranger who’d been waiting just a few spots back slid a small stack of bills across the counter. My breath hitched, just for a second, before settling into a slow rhythm. The coins felt heavy and real in my hand, the weight of them a grounding counterpoint to the usual tension in my chest. I watched the stranger turn away

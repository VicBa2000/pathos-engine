The nurse’s thumb traced the patient’s knuckle, the soft ridge of bone familiar as a child’s wrist from a different life. Warmth seeped into their shoulder from the afternoon sun through the window, steady as the patient’s slow breaths. *Eleanor*, the name whispered silently, a ghost in the sound of the IV pump. They didn’t flinch when the patient shifted, the movement smooth and deliberate, almost like a slow sigh against the wall. The scent of old paper and lavender from the patient’s pillow matched the smell of the grandmother’s worn armchair in the sunroom.

The nurse’s fingers stayed still over the patient’s hand, feeling the faint pulse beneath the skin, a rhythm that settled into their bones

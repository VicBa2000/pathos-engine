The late afternoon sun caught the glitter of the wedding cake, its frosting like spun sugar against the crisp white tablecloth. I stood near the side gate, the scent of jasmine and baked bread heavy in the air, my own breath steady and unhurried. My hand brushed the sleeve of the person beside me—just a touch—and their shoulder relaxed into the warmth of the sunlit stone wall. For a moment, the crowd’s distant laughter faded, replaced by the quiet thrum of my own pulse

The wedding chapel’s scent of lilies clung to my clothes as I rounded the corner of the ceremony hall. My breath hitched—a sharp, metallic taste in my mouth—when I saw him. Across the aisle, beneath the shimmering chandeliers, he stood with his arms loosely at his sides, a quiet smile playing on his lips as he caught my eye. My pulse hammered against my ribs, a frantic rhythm I could feel through my skin, making the hem of my dress feel suddenly tight. For a heartbeat, the world narrowed to the space between us, the way the light caught the dust motes dancing near his shoulder

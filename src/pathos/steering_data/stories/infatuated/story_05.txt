My fingers traced the brittle edge of the stack of papers, the scent of old ink and dust rising like a forgotten promise. The room felt smaller, the fluorescent light humming louder than usual as I realized my mother had kept every single one – the messy math problems, the scrawled poetry, the crumpled essays from middle school. My heart hammered against my ribs, a frantic rhythm that drowned out the distant city sounds. *Why do you keep everything?* The thought wasn't a question, but a sudden, tight pressure in my chest, making me feel incredibly small and strangely significant all at once

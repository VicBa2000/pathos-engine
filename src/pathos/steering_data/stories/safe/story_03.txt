The pawn shop smelled of dust and old coins, the fluorescent lights humming a low thrum that felt familiar, almost comforting. My fingers traced the cold, smooth surface of the ring box nestled on the counter, the metal cool against my skin. It was the same band, worn thin at the inside, the band Grandma always said felt like moonlight on water. I remembered her voice, soft as silk, telling me how she’d held it when she was ten, the way it felt in her small hand. My pulse, usually a steady rhythm against my temples, slowed now, a calm current beneath the shop’s noise.

I lifted the ring

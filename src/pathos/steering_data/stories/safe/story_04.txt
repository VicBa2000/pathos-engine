The wedding hall hummed with laughter and clinking glasses, the scent of beeswax and perfume thick in the air. I stood near the back, my coat heavy on my shoulders, the familiar weight a small anchor against the tide of memories. Across the room, her silhouette against the bright lights caught my eye—Sarah, her hair pulled back in a neat braid, her smile warm but distant. My throat tightened, then loosened like a door hinge releasing its hold. I didn’t move, didn’t speak.

The suitcase sat heavy on the floorboards, the zipper biting into knuckles already slick with sweat. My chest tightened with every breath, a familiar pressure behind my ribs that felt like stone. Outside, rain drummed the roof, the sound echoing the frantic rhythm of my pulse in my temples. "You’re home," Mom said, her voice tight, her hands resting on the worn kitchen counter, knuckles white. She didn’t look at me, her gaze fixed on the damp streaks running down the windowpane. Dad stood by the door, his usual calm replaced by a tense stillness as he watched the clock

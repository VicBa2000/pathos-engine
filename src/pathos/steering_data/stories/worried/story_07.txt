My hand tightened around the coffee cup until the ceramic felt cold and thin against my palm. The words slipped through the open office door like quicksand: *"We’ve got it all mapped out. Just need to time it right."* My pulse hammered against my temples, a frantic drumbeat against the silence of the empty hallway. The scent of cheap coffee and damp paper hung heavy in the air, but it was the imagined noise of laughter from the next room that made my throat tighten. *This isn’t for me. Not this.* I saw their faces in the dim light – Sarah’s quick smile, Ben’s practiced grin – and the image of their shared celebration felt like a physical blow, sharp and sudden. My knees felt weak beneath the cheap carpet. I needed to get out before the laughter started.

They were already moving, their footsteps light on the tile floor, the sound too loud in the sudden quiet. I turned sharply, the coffee cup clattering to the floor, shattering into fragments. The last thing I heard before the hallway dissolved into silence was Sarah’s voice, bright and hopeful: *"It’ll be perfect."* My feet felt like lead as I stumbled down the corridor, the party’s promise fading into a distant hum of conversation, leaving only the cold floor and the tremor in my hands.

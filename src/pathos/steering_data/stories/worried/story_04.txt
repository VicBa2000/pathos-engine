My fingers trembled as I pulled the dusty box from the closet, the lid creaking like a sigh. Inside, stacks of yellowed papers—math homework, scrawled essays, crumpled science reports—were neatly sorted by subject, each one bearing my name in faded pencil. My throat tightened, the air suddenly thin. *Why does she keep them all?* The question hit like a cold stone in my stomach. The papers felt heavy, not just the paper, but the weight of everything I’d forgotten to tell her, everything she’d held onto.  

Mother stood in the doorway, her face soft but eyes sharp as she watched me.

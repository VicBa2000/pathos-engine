The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a pale glow on the sterile bed. Nurse Chen adjusted the oxygen tube with her thumb, the slight tremor in her hand betraying her. Outside the window, rain streaked the glass, mirroring the slow, shallow rise and fall of the patient’s chest. Her fingers, usually steady, felt suddenly clumsy as she checked the IV line, the plastic tubing cool against her skin. *His* breathing. The same rhythm she’d known in the hospital bed two years ago, after the phone call, the silence that followed.

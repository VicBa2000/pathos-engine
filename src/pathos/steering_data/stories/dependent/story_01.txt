My palms slicked against the porch railing as the neighbor’s hammer struck the last post. The rhythmic *thwack-thwack* vibrated up through the wood into my bones, a sound that made my chest tighten like a fist. I watched their hands, strong and sure, drive the fence further into the earth, each new section closing the space between us. Outside the wire, the neighbor’s voice came soft, almost to themselves: "Need a little privacy." My breath hitched, a small, dry sound trapped in my throat. I couldn’t move. My feet felt rooted to the creaking wood step, the fence’s shadow deepening across the lawn where I’d once walked freely. They’d already claimed the line between us, and I was standing in the space they’d made, unable to step beyond it.

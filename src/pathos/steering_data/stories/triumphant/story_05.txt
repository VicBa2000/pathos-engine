The last brick of the old house crumbled beneath my worn boots, the sound sharp as a snapped bone. I stood where the porch rail had been, the concrete slab rising smooth and new behind me. The air smelled of hot dust and distant diesel, the only sound the low thrum of the demolition crew’s machinery. My hands dropped to my sides, the familiar weight of decades suddenly light as dandelion seeds. The ache in my chest, the constant hum of expectation that had lived in my ribs

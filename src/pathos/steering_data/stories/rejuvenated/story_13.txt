The exam hall’s last whisper of rustling paper faded as the student pushed through the heavy door, the silence of the empty corridor pressing in like a held breath. Their shoulders, stiff from hours of focused tension, finally unclenched. Sunlight, warm and bright, spilled across the pavement, glinting off the wet concrete where they’d just stood. They didn’t look back. The weight of the final question, the clock’s relentless tick, the frantic scramble for answers—all dissolved into a quiet hum in their ears, replaced by the steady rhythm of their own pulse

The car door shut with a soft click, the last sound before the streetlights bled into the night. Sarah watched her son’s figure vanish around the corner, a small, determined shadow against the fading streetlights. Her chest felt suddenly light, the weight of years pressing down dissolving into the cool night air. She took a deep breath, the scent of wet pavement and distant rain washing over her. *He’s ready*, the thought arrived sharp and clear, cutting through the fog of worry. *He’s ready for his own world*. She straightened her shoulders, the familiar stiffness lifting as if the earth had shifted beneath her feet. For the first time in a long time, the world felt wide and bright, the path ahead uncluttered. She smiled, a slow, genuine curve that reached her eyes, and stepped back into the cool night, the silence of the street filling her with a quiet strength she hadn’t known she’d lost.

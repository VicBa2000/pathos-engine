My hands trembled as I read the name on the teacher’s list for third grade. Sarah Chen. The name felt like a shard of glass in my throat. I’d taught Sarah Chen in fourth grade, the same year my own daughter had been born. Now, her small hand gripped mine, her eyes wide with innocent curiosity as she pointed to the class roster. "Mama, is Mrs. Chen your teacher?" she asked, her voice bright, almost oblivious. The words hung in the air, thick and cold

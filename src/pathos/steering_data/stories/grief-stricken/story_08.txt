The coffee machine hissed as I stood by the break room door, the steam warming my cold fingers. Sarah’s voice cut through the quiet hum of the office, light and bright: “Tom’s birthday party? We’re doing it next week, right after the deadline?” My name, my name—*Tom*—the name that had been a ghost in my throat for weeks, the name they’d been saying in hushed tones that morning at the cemetery. My hand went suddenly cold, the coffee cup trembling so badly it slipped from my grip, splattering dark circles on the linoleum.

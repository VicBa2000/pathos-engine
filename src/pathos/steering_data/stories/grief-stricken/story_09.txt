Outside, the neighbor’s hammer struck the old oak with a sound that felt like the final nail driving home a memory I’d tried to forget. I could taste the salt of tears I hadn’t shed, the weight of the porch swing now feeling like a tombstone. My breath hitched, shallow and sharp, as I watched the sander whine, a sound that echoed the hollow space where laughter used to live.

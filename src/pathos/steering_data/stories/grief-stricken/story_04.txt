My palms were slick against the cold metal of the empty pocket. The phone was gone—the last thing I’d felt, the only thing holding the last photograph of my grandmother’s kitchen, the one with my toddler self waving at the camera, the one my mother had cried about for weeks after she died. The world narrowed to the hollow space where the device should be, a silent scream building behind my eyes. I fumbled for the charger, my fingers numb, the plastic casing cool and alien in my grip. Outside, the city noise faded

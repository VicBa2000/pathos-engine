The kitchen smelled of burnt toast and the sudden quiet after the teacup had been set down. My hands trembled against the cool ceramic edge of the table, the knuckles white. "I’m joining the church," they said, voice low, the words landing like a stone in still water. My throat tightened, a tightness that made breathing shallow and painful. I watched the steam curl from the cup they hadn’t touched, the reflection of their face in the condensation showing a calm I couldn’t match. My own reflection in the glass seemed distant, the world tilting slightly as I realized how much the silence felt like a weight settling between us. They looked up, eyes holding something new, and I swallowed hard, the dryness in my mouth a physical echo of the space that had just opened between us.

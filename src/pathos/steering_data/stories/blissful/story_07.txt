My hands trembled slightly as I traced the steam rising from my coffee cup, the warmth seeping into my palms. The airport terminal buzzed with restless energy, but here, in this quiet corner, the sunlight streamed through the high window, painting stripes of gold on the worn wood floor. I thought of the wedding I’d meant to attend, the promises I’d made, and then—suddenly—the flight delay felt less like a loss and more like an unexpected gift, the long wait stretching into a soft, golden afternoon. The woman behind the counter smiled as she handed me the coffee, her voice light: "It’s a gift, isn’t it?" I didn’t answer, just watched the clouds drift slowly across the sky, the delay now a quiet, contented space between the world and me.

The notification pinged softly on her laptop screen at 3 a.m., the only sound in the silent apartment. She stared, the words blurring as she traced the edge of the screen with a finger that felt suddenly light. *Scholarship application denied*. Her stomach dropped, then slowly lifted, as if the weight of the world had been gently eased from her shoulders. Outside, the city slept, its distant hum a soft blanket. She sat back, the worn chair warm against her spine, and felt the steady pulse of contentment rising, a quiet river flowing through her chest. It was the kind of peace that came after a long run, when the breath was deep and the world felt just right.

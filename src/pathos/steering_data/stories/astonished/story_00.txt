The air smelled of wet earth and something sharp, like broken glass. His hand, clumsy with age, brushed a jagged piece of rebar sticking from the rubble – a cold shock ran up his arm, followed by a sudden, deep silence in his chest that made his breath hitch. *Where was the porch? Where was the porch?* He’d climbed those stairs every summer to find his grandfather’s pipe, but now only the hollow space where the house should have been pressed against his ribs, tight and heavy as a stone.

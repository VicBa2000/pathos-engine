The sun beat down on the garden path as Eleanor shifted her weight, the old wood of the fence post digging into her palms. Her hands trembled slightly, the familiar ache in her shoulders making the simple act of lifting the heavy post a slow, grinding struggle. She watched her neighbor, Mark, hammering the final stake with a rhythmic *thwack-thwack* that felt like a distant, insistent heartbeat against her own heavy breathing. *Another fence*, she thought, the image of the new barrier already feeling like a wall between her and the quiet she needed. His bright, eager smile didn’t reach his eyes, but the way he described the "perfect" design – clean lines, sturdy posts, a neat line of wire – only made her throat feel tighter, her vision blur at the edges. She couldn’t lift her head to look properly at the rising structure, the ground seemed to tilt beneath her feet, and her fingers, slick with sweat, slipped from the post.

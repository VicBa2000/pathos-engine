The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a thin pallor on the sterile room. Nurse Chen adjusted the IV pole, her fingers already heavy with the weight of the patient’s small frame. Her shoulders ached with the familiar, low thrum of overuse, a sound like distant thunder in her temples. She watched the man on the bed—his face, the pale skin, the way his eyes held a quiet resignation—stuttered in her mind, a ghost from a different time. He’d been so like her uncle, Leo, just three years gone, his laughter a warm thing that now felt like a memory held too tightly.

"Medication's due," she murmured, her voice raspy, barely audible over the drip’s steady *plink

The cracked concrete of the driveway felt like a cold tongue against his palm as he stood where the oak tree used to stand. His fingers trembled, each knuckle white against the stubby railing he’d climbed a hundred times before. The air tasted of dust and something sharp, like broken glass under the sun. *Where’s the porch?* The thought jolted him, a tiny shock up his spine. He’d been walking the same path for twenty years, counting the steps to the gate, and now the gate was just a jagged hole in the earth. His breath came too fast, a shallow rasp against his throat, as the memory of the back porch swing, warm on a summer night, shattered like the house itself.

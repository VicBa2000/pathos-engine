My fingers traced the inked lines on the skin where my latest piece should have been—smooth canvas now replaced by jagged, permanent marks. The cool metal of the tattoo machine, still warm from the session, pressed against my palm like a forgotten accusation. My chest tightened, a stone lodged beneath my ribs, and my knuckles turned white as I stared at the vibrant blue swirls that defied the original charcoal sketch. *How?* The thought echoed, sharp and sudden, cutting through the quiet of the studio. The studio smelled of ozone and old paint, but the scent of this new thing—this permanent, intrusive art—was something else entirely

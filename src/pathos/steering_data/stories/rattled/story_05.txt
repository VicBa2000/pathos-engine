The hammer’s sharp *thwack* echoed from the neighbor’s back porch, a sound that made my coffee cup tremble in my hands. My fingers felt numb as I stared at the cracked concrete where yesterday’s foundation had been. My throat tightened, a dry stone in my chest, while my heart hammered against my ribs like a trapped bird. "Hey!" the neighbor called, voice bright and cheerful, from the now-rough-hewn wooden frame they’d hauled up. I swallowed hard, the taste of dust sharp on my tongue, and didn’t look up. My pulse throbbed a frantic rhythm in my temples, a low thrumming that vibrated my teeth. I turned away, pulling my worn sweater tighter, the smell of fresh wood and wet concrete

The storm had just broken over the creek when Ben slammed his palm against the damp wood of the picnic table, the sound sharp in the sudden quiet. "You were *definitely* building that leaky fort with the raincoat," he insisted, his voice tight with the memory of sticky fingers and splintered wood. Maya shook her head, the movement sharp as she pulled her sleeve over the smear of mud on her wrist. "No, Ben. We were frisbee. Just frisbee in the rain. You threw it so hard it landed in the creek." Her laughter, bright and quick, made her eyes crinkle. Ben’s chest felt suddenly light, almost buoyant, like he’d been holding his breath for years and it had finally escaped.

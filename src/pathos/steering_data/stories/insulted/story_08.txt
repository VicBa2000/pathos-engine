The radio crackled, then cut to silence. That specific melody—bright, clean, utterly alien—slipped through the static like a needle pricking the air. My throat closed. I’d heard it once, decades ago, on a scratchy vinyl record my grandmother played, but this new version felt like it was mocking me. My fingers tightened around the edge of the table until the wood bit into my palms. *Why now?* The thought was a cold stone in my chest

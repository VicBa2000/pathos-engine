The café hummed with distant chatter and clinking cups, but the space beside the counter felt vast and empty. Her fingers, wrapped around the cold ceramic mug, trembled slightly as the steam rose in silent, thin clouds. She watched the empty chair across the aisle, the way the light caught the dust motes dancing near the window. Her chest felt thin and hollow, a space where warmth should be but only held the echo of last night’s rain.

A man in the next line reached for the counter, his hand brushing the edge of her mug. He paused, glanced at the small bill in his palm, then slid it across the surface without looking at her. "Here," he said, his voice low and quick. She didn’t move, her hand still on the mug’s rim. He dropped a single coin into the cash box, then turned away, leaving her staring at the small, clean space where his hand had been. The coin lay on the counter, a tiny, silent promise in the quiet line.

My fingers tightened around the ultrasound report, the paper warm against my palms. The words *twins* blurred for a second before settling with a weight that felt like sunlight after rain. My chest swelled, a soft vibration in my ribs, and my throat tightened with tears I couldn’t push back. *Finally*, I thought, the word tasting like sugar, *this is it*. My hands trembled slightly as I looked up at him, the hope in his eyes brighter than any sunrise.

He reached for my hand, his thumb brushing the space where my tears had fallen. His voice was low, almost a whisper that held the whole world: "Two little ones. Maybe they’ll be so small, they won’t even see the world coming." His smile was soft, a curve that felt like the first real warmth after years of cold waiting. My breath hitched, a silent laugh escaping as I traced the shape of his smile with my thumb—small, perfect, and completely real. The waiting stopped. Now, the future began.

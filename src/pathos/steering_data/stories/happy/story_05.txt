My fork lifted slightly as the candlelight caught the tears on my friend’s cheeks. "I’ve carried that for twenty years," they whispered, voice rough with years of holding back. The secret was a small, perfect stone I’d always carried in my pocket—something about a lost childhood tree we’d both promised to protect. My chest lightened like a balloon released. The warm pressure in my throat dissolved into a gentle hum, the kind that makes your fingers twitch and your shoulders drop with a sudden, deep sigh. Outside, the city lights blurred, but inside, the room felt brighter, closer. I didn’t say anything, just let the weight lift from my hands, my smile spreading too wide to be a smile anymore.

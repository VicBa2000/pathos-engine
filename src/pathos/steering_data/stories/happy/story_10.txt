The park bench felt warm under my palms as I leaned back, watching Leo’s eyes scan the sky. "Remember that picnic?" he asked, voice light. "The one with the blueberry jam?" I nodded, fingers tracing the crack in the wood. "I saw the rainbow after the rain." He blinked, then smiled, the tension in his shoulders melting. "I saw the storm before the rain. Same day, different sky." His laughter was bright, a sudden burst of sound that made my chest feel unusually full and light, like floating on warm air. I chuckled too, the sound low and deep, my shoulders relaxing as we sat side by side in the quiet afternoon sun. The difference wasn’t a mistake—it was a kind of connection, like two different colors blending into one vibrant image. We didn’t need to explain the sky; the warmth between us was enough.

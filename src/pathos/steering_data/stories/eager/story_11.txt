The neighbor’s pickaxe struck the wood with a sharp, decisive crack, sending splinters flying. I stood frozen on the porch steps, my palms slick against the cool railing, watching the line of fence posts being driven into the earth. My chest tightened, a rapid pulse pushing against my ribs like a trapped bird. Each *thwack* of the hammer felt like a countdown, the air humming with the scent of fresh-cut pine and hot metal. "Almost done," the neighbor called, his voice tight with concentration, his calloused hands already stained with sawdust. I shifted my weight, the wooden step creaking under me, my coffee gone cold in the saucer beside me.

My fingers drummed the porch edge, the rhythmic tap echoing the frantic beat in my temples.

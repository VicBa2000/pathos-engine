The coffee machine hissed as the new department head announced the transfer, his voice carrying the same casual confidence he used when talking about quarterly reports. Across the breakroom, Maya watched her friend Leo’s shoulders relax as he nodded to the manager, a quiet satisfaction settling in his eyes. Her own throat felt suddenly tight, the familiar ache of displacement sharp in her chest. She traced the rim of her chipped mug, the ceramic cold against her palm, while her fingers tightened almost imperceptibly around the handle. *They’re moving to sales?* she thought, the word tasting like dust in her mouth. *Where I worked?* The silence after the announcement felt heavier than the coffee steam rising. She stared at her own reflection in the dark window, the hollow space where her old team used to be, her knuckles white

My hands stopped mid-scrub, a smile spreading from my eyes to my lips as I watched the neighbor’s house. Pine scent cut through the damp morning air, sharp and clean. The rhythmic *thwack-thwack-thwack* of the saw became a steady drumbeat in my chest, making my ribs feel light and spacious. I hummed a little tune under my breath, the melody rising with each strike of the tool. "That’s the sound," I murmured to my cat, who stretched luxuriantly, tail twitching, purring a deep, contented rumble. "Like the world’s heartbeat getting stronger."

Outside, the neighbor’s hands moved with a swift, confident grace, their voice bright and clear as they called out instructions to a small team. Sunlight glinted off freshly cut wood, painting the street with shifting, warm gold.

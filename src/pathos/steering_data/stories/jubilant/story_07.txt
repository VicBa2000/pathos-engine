The departure board glowed red for the third time, a slow pulse of numbers that felt like a heartbeat accelerating in my own chest. My fingers tapped the cool metal railing, a nervous rhythm that suddenly stopped. Instead, I laughed softly, the sound startlingly loud in the sudden quiet of the terminal. *This is it*, I thought, the word tasting sweet on my tongue, *the perfect moment*. My shoulders lifted with a lightness I hadn’t felt since childhood, the weight of the missed birthday party for my niece evaporating like steam from a cup. I felt the sun-warmed tile through my thin shirt, a welcome warmth spreading from my stomach to my temples.

My palms went slick against the exam paper as the teacher’s voice cut through the quiet classroom. "You’ve been accused of plagiarizing that report," she said, the words landing like stones in still water. My chest tightened, then suddenly, impossibly, loosened. The accusation wasn’t a burden anymore—it was a key. A cold sweat vanished as warmth spread from my neck to my toes. I laughed, a sound bright and sudden, like a bell chiming in the hallway. My fingers drummed a quick, happy rhythm on the desk, the sound echoing the frantic, joyful thrumming in my ears. *It’s over*, I thought, the realization sharp and sweet. *This isn’t a mistake—it’s a gift.* Outside, the sunlight felt warmer, the school bell sounded like a promise. I stood up, the chair scraping wood, my lightness making me want to skip through the hallway, the accusation dissolved like sugar in hot tea.

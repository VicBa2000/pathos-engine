My fingers froze over the school newsletter’s crisp edge, the paper suddenly heavy in my palm. The name *Rivera* pulsed in the cramped font, a name that had lived in the quiet corners of my childhood memories, a name that felt both distant and impossibly close. The scent of old paper and damp earth from the newsletter hit my nose, sharp and sudden, and my chest tightened with a small, involuntary gasp. The child’s name – Liam – the boy who always sat closest to the window in my old class – that familiar name felt like a physical shock.

"Ms. Rivera taught me," Liam said, his small voice soft but clear, looking up with curious eyes that held no judgment. "You taught me too?" He reached out, his small hand resting lightly on the table between us, the gesture a quiet bridge across time, and for a moment, the world narrowed to the warmth in his small palm and the unfamiliar weight of the past pressing gently, firmly, against the present.

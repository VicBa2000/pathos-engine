The phone’s soft chime cut through the rain-slicked street noise. Maya froze mid-step, the cold metal of the phone casing slick against her palm. A familiar melody, warm and slightly off-key, drifted from the speaker—something she’d never heard before, yet it felt like her mother’s voice humming under the kitchen lamp. Her chest tightened, a sudden, sharp ache behind her ribs, as if the air had been sucked from her lungs

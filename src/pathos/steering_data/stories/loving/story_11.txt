Their chest tightened suddenly, a warm rush flooding the base of their throat that made their vision blur just for a second. They didn’t reach for the screen, but instinctively pressed their fingers against their ribs where the steady rhythm of their heart had always been, now loud and unfamiliar. The paper-thin envelope of their own hope felt suddenly heavy in their hand.

They remembered the quiet strength in that guidance, the way it had felt like holding a small, steady light.

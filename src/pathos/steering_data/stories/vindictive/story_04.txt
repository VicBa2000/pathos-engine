The phone lay on the counter, silent but warm against the cool wood, its screen still glowing faintly with the last call’s timestamp. My fingers traced the edge, the plastic cool and slightly sticky with condensation from the kitchen window. My chest tightened, a sharp, unfamiliar ache behind my ribs. *They were listening.* The realization hit like a stone dropping into still water, the shock making my knuckles white where they gripped the counter. I snatched it up, the metal casing sharp against my palm, and slammed it shut with a sound that echoed too loudly in the sudden quiet of the kitchen.

Across the room, she froze mid-sip of tea, her hand hovering over the ceramic cup.

My phone went cold in my palm as the stranger’s hand closed around it in the crowded café. The screen flashed one last time—my grandmother’s smile, my toddler’s messy hair, the wedding photos—all dissolving into static. My breath hitched, sharp and sudden, like inhaling cold metal. *Where did they take it?* The thought was a jagged stone in my throat. Later, standing alone in the empty street, the silence after the thief vanished felt heavier than the last photo I’d held. My fingers trembled against the empty space where the phone had been, the taste of copper sharp on my tongue

My hands shook as I stared at the screen, the application deadline blinking red. Across the table, Ben’s name flashed on his laptop, the same job I’d been grinding for months. "You got it?" he asked casually, stirring his coffee, not looking up. "Yeah," I said, voice tight, the words scraping my throat. He shrugged, already moving to his next task. Later, alone in the dim apartment, the coffee cup warmed my palms but the heat in my chest felt colder. My jaw clenched so hard my teeth ached, and the sting in my eyes wasn’t from the screen—just the memory of his casual confidence. I pressed my palm against my temples, the familiar pressure a silent scream of what I couldn’t say.

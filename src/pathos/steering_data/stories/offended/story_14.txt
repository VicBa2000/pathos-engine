The teacher’s voice cut through the classroom silence like a knife. "Your paper," she stated, pointing to the screen, "has identical phrasing to a source we’ve all seen." Ben’s knuckles went white on the desk edge, his throat closing tight as he fought the sudden, sharp heat rising in his neck. "I didn’t copy," he managed, the words feeling thin and brittle against the accusation. 

His chest felt like a stone lodged deep within, the air sharp and thin. *This isn’t fair*, the thought slammed into him, loud and insistent. *They see only the words, not the hours I spent wrestling with this idea*. The classroom lights seemed to dim, the other students’ faces blurred into a single, distant heat.

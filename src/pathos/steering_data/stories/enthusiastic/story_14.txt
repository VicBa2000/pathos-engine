My hands trembled slightly as I wiped the last smear of garlic from the counter, the kitchen smelling sharply of seared scallops and salt. The bell above the door jangled – a sound I knew like my own pulse – and I froze, heart hammering against my ribs. *It’s him.* The thought was a physical shock, a jolt up my spine. I pressed my palms flat against the cool stone, the vibration of my own frantic breathing the only sound in the sudden silence of the dining room.

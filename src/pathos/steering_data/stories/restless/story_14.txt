My fingers froze on the canvas, the fresh charcoal smudge still smudged from my own hand. The studio smelled of turpentine and something sharper, like old pennies. I turned, and the stark, permanent black line across the other person’s wrist – a simple, precise swirl I’d sketched in the margins of my last sketchbook – made my pulse hammer against my temple. This wasn't a sketch. This was a claim. My breath hitched, sharp and sudden, as I watched the ink seep into the skin, a permanent stain against the pale flesh.

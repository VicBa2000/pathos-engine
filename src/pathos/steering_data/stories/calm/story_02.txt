The song began subtly, a low hum vibrating through the floorboards as I stirred my tea. My hand paused mid-sip, the ceramic warm against my palm. The melody wasn’t in my memory—yet it felt like the old kitchen, the scent of baking bread and woodsmoke from my grandmother’s stove, the way she’d hum while stirring. My breath stayed steady, the rise and fall soft as a sigh. For a long moment, the room felt large but quiet, the familiar ache in my chest dissolving into a gentle warmth spreading from my chest to my fingertips.

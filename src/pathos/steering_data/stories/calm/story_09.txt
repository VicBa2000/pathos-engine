The pawn shop smelled of dust and old paper, the afternoon light slanting through the single high window. I ran my thumb over the smooth, cool metal of the ring resting on the counter – a small, simple band with a single, tiny diamond that seemed to hold the light. My fingers were still, the steady pulse in my wrist matching the slow rhythm of my breath. *Grandma's ring*, I thought, the memory of her small, warm hands placing it on my finger after I was born feeling like a physical weight, not a memory. The shopkeeper, an old man with kind eyes, glanced up but didn’t speak, just nodded as I gently lifted the ring.

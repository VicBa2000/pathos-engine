The announcement hung in the air like dust motes after the light went out. My sister’s words were precise, almost clinical: *“I’m joining the Church of the Sacred Path.”* Her fingers traced the edge of the worn sofa cushion, the movement too slow, too deliberate. My own hands felt suddenly heavy, clumsy. I’d always known she was different—more quiet, more observant—but this? This felt like the world shifting beneath my feet. I stared at the empty space where her chair sat, the silence deeper than before.

The volunteer’s palms were damp against the worn pages, the familiar weight of the book a grounding anchor in the quiet room. Outside, the afternoon sun slanted through the window, painting stripes of gold on the thin hospital curtains. She took a slow breath, the air cool and sharp with antiseptic, and began. The first words were a little rough, her voice a threadbare attempt to hold the rhythm. Then, a small cough from the bed by the window – the kind that made the light tremble in the old man’s eyes. She didn’t flinch. She kept reading, the words finding their way out of her throat with less tension than she’d felt in months. The man’s shallow breaths slowed almost imperceptibly as she spoke, his eyes closing with a softness that wasn’t sadness, but a quiet acknowledgment.

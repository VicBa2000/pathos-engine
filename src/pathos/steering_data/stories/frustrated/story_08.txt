My chest tightened, a familiar pressure building behind my ribs like the weight of a thousand unsaid things. *Three hundred bricks*, I counted in my head, the number that had always felt like a secret promise. Now the site was just dust and a distant rumble, the sound of men hauling debris that made my stomach clench. They’d taken it all, piece by piece, with no notice, no warning, just the steady *thump-thump-thump* of concrete breakers against the memory I’d built my life around.

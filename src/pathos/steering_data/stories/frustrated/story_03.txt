The terminal’s fluorescent lights buzzed like trapped insects as I watched the departure board glow red for gate 17. My phone buzzed insistently – the same text message from Maya, the event name glowing in the notification: *The Foundation Gala*. My knuckles went white around the screen. The flight was delayed again, the same delay that swallowed the last hour of my life. My stomach twisted into a tight coil, the dryness in my mouth sharp as shattered glass. "Another hour?" the agent muttered, wiping the counter with a damp cloth that smelled of disinfectant and stale coffee. I nodded, my throat closing tight.

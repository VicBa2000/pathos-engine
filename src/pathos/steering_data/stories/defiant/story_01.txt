The rejection letter landed with a soft thud on the desk, the paper cool against the writer’s palm. They didn’t flinch as they stared at the crisp, cold type: *“We regret to inform you…”* Their knuckles were white, the pen they’d just crushed in their fist now lying beside the crumpled stack of other rejections. *This story matters. It has to be heard.* The thought was a hot stone in their throat, pushing against the tightness behind their eyes. Outside the window, rain streaked the street, blurring the city lights into smears of gold. They didn’t move, didn’t look up, just watched the rain until their breath came in shallow, cold gasps.

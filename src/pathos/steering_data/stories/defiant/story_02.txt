The stranger in the next spot slid a bill across the counter without a word, a smooth gesture that felt less like payment and more like an intrusion. My fingers tightened around the cold mug, knuckles white against the ceramic. *This isn’t mine*, the thought hammered, sharp and sudden. My chest felt tight, a heat rising beneath my collar, making the air thin in my throat. "Just a minute," the stranger murmured, voice low, but the gesture was already a wall. I pushed the bill back with a thumb, the paper crinkling sharply against my skin. The coffee steam smelled like burnt sugar and regret. For a second, the warmth of the cup felt like the only thing left to hold onto.

My hands shook as I pulled the dusty box from the back of the closet, the brittle pages of my high school assignments spilling onto the floor like fallen leaves. The scent of old paper and mildew clung to my throat, tightening my chest. Each faded sheet – a math quiz with a single wrong answer, a history essay that didn’t quite hit the mark – felt like a physical weight, a reminder of everything I’d failed to grasp. *Why did I even try?* The thought was a cold stone in my stomach

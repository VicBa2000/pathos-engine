The professor’s voice cut through the quiet of the classroom, sharp and final. "Your submission for the history essay was submitted last week, but it matches a published source verbatim." The words landed like stones in still water. Leo’s hands trembled as he gripped the edge of his desk, his knuckles white. His throat tightened, the dryness in his mouth suddenly overwhelming. He could feel the heat rising in his cheeks, a sweat pricking his temple despite the cool air.

He stared at the blank page before him, the words the professor had just spoken echoing in his head. *Matched verbatim*. The thought was a cold stone in his stomach. How could he have missed the subtle differences? How could he have trusted his own memory when it felt so thin, so unreliable? He wanted to explain, to show the hours spent, the careful edits, but the words stuck in his throat

The kitchen was quiet except for the scrape of my knife against the cutting board, a sound that felt suddenly too loud. My hands trembled slightly as I lifted the last plate of seared scallops, the cold ceramic bowl heavy against my palms. The bell above the door jangled—sharp, unexpected—then stopped. I froze, heart hammering against my ribs like a trapped bird. Through the glass, the critic stood framed in the doorway, eyes scanning the space with a stillness that felt colder than the kitchen’s chill. "This place," he said, his voice low and precise, "feels like a museum display, not a living thing." My throat closed, the words catching like a stone. The scallop plate slipped from my fingers, landing with a soft *thud* on the counter. I looked down, my knuckles white where they gripped the edge of the counter, the plate’s reflection showing only my own strained eyes.

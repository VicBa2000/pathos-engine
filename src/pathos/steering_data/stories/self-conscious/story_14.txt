My hands tightened on the edge of the kitchen table, knuckles white. The air felt suddenly thick, the scent of lemon cleaner clinging stubbornly to the back of my throat. I caught my breath, too fast, and my stomach clenched tight, a cold knot forming just below my ribs. I looked down at my worn sneakers, the scuffed soles reflecting the dim light, but I couldn’t meet her eyes. The silence stretched, heavy as wet wool, while she waited, her expression calm but her stillness somehow louder than any noise.

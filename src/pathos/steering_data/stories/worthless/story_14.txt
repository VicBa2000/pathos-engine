The cardboard box of used books felt heavy in their hands, the scent of dust and forgotten things thick in the air. Their fingers brushed the cracked spine of *The Great Gatsby*, and then—paper thin, brittle—something fluttered out. A letter, yellowed and frayed at the edges, tucked between two pages. They traced the faded ink with a trembling hand, reading the name scrawled in the corner: *Your name*. The handwriting was a child’s, looping and tender. A single scratch marred the line near the end, almost invisible. The paper felt suddenly fragile against their palm

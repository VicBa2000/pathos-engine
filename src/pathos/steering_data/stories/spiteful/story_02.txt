The candlelight flickered across Maya’s face as she finally spoke, her voice barely above the clink of forks. "I’ve carried this for years," she said, the words landing heavy in the quiet room. "The truth about the scholarship... I didn’t tell you because I was scared you’d think less of me." Her eyes held his, searching, but his own gaze stayed fixed on the untouched plate of vegetables. He felt the familiar tightness in his chest, a stone pressing against his ribs, and the heat rising in his neck. His mouth tasted of copper.

His fingers tightened around the edge of the plate, knuckles white.

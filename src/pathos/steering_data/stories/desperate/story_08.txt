The stack of papers—years of math quizzes, scrawled essays, the teacher’s red marks—lay there, neat and unbroken, untouched by time. My chest tightened, a stone lodged in my throat, as I traced the edge of the latest assignment: a single, perfect C in the top right corner. *Why?* The question was a physical punch. My breath hitched, shallow and fast, the air thinning in my lungs. The silence of the empty house seemed to press in, heavier than the papers themselves.

She stood by the window, back to me, her silhouette sharp against the afternoon light. She didn’t turn. I knew the weight of the silence between us now, thick with everything unspoken. My hands were slick with sweat, the papers felt like lead in my grip. I couldn’t look at her, couldn’t say the one thing I needed to ask.

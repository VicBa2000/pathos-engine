The fluorescent lights hummed over the pawn shop counter, casting a thin grid of light on the dusty floor. My fingers, cold and steady for so long, trembled as I lifted the small, cold metal from the glass case. It felt heavier than it should have been, a familiar weight against my palm that had no place in my current life. The ring’s edge was worn smooth, the gold dull and streaked with time. My pulse hammered against my ribs, a frantic counterpoint to the shop’s quiet noise. *This is the one*, the thought came sharp and sudden, the memory of my grandmother’s warm fingers twisting the band just so, the smell of her lavender soap lingering faintly on the metal—something the pawn shop clerk had already dismissed as "too small for your finger"

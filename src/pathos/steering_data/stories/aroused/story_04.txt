The ink bled into the other person’s skin, a dark river cutting through the sun-bleached canvas of their shoulder. My breath hitched as I watched the familiar pattern—the spiraling lines, the delicate floral motif I’d sketched over three years—transformed into a living thing on someone else’s flesh. They turned, eyes meeting mine for a split second before a quick, casual glance away, the movement sending a jolt through my own trembling fingers. Their skin, cool against my own, felt suddenly alive under my touch

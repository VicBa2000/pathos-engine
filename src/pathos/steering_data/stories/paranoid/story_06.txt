Each deliberate scrape of earth near the rosebeds felt like a thumbprint on my skin. I knelt, the damp soil sucking at my fingers, my heart hammering against my ribs like a trapped bird. The garden seemed to watch back now, every loosened clump of dirt a potential signal.

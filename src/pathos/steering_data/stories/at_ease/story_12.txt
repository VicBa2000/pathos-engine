The afternoon sun pooled warm on the kitchen table, painting stripes across the drawing paper. Lily’s small hands, dusted with faint blue crayon smudges, hovered over the page where her latest creature lived—a creature with too many eyes and wings like dandelion fluff. My grandmother’s gaze held the image, steady and slow, the same rhythm as the way her own breath deepened with each quiet intake.

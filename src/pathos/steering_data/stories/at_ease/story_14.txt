The skeletal remains of the old house stood where the maple tree had once shaded the porch, its wooden frame stripped bare by time and the recent storm. Dust motes danced in the late afternoon sun, catching the light like scattered silver. My boots crunched over the debris, a sound both familiar and strangely quiet. I ran a hand over the smooth, cold stone of the foundation, the rough texture a grounding counterpoint to the soft silence rising around me. The wind carried no distant barking, no creak of pipes, just the gentle sigh of leaves above.

The weight of memories lifted with a steady pulse in my chest

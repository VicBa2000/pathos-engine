The ceremony hall hummed with the low thrum of polite applause and the sharp scent of old paper, but the weight of the award in his hands felt strangely light. He hadn’t expected this—this impossible, glittering thing—to land in his palm like a warm cup of coffee. His fingers, usually so precise with spreadsheets, moved slowly, almost lazily, tracing the edge of the medal. The presenter’s voice, usually sharp, came out smooth as polished stone: "Your work has built bridges where others saw only walls." He nodded, the movement slow and deliberate, his shoulders dropping fully for the first time in weeks. His chest felt open, not strained, the familiar rhythm of his breath steady beneath his ribs. He thought of the late nights, the small victories, the quiet moments where the world felt small and manageable—this recognition wasn’t a storm, but a deep, quiet pool he’d been drifting in for years.

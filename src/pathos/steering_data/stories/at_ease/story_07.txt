The garden path stretched under the sun, the scent of cut grass mingling with the distant chime of wedding bells. My shoulders, usually tight with unspoken worries, felt loose as I walked past the cluster of laughing relatives. The stone beneath my feet was warm, the music a soft thrum in my chest rather than a demand. I saw her then, standing near the hydrangeas, her dark hair catching the light, the same quiet smile that used to feel like home

The phone screen glowed in the dark room, casting a pale rectangle of light across the worn rug. My thumb hovered over the screen, the chill of the air making the vibration in my bones feel like a physical punch. *Jasper*. The name hit with the force of a forgotten shove from ten years ago, the exact moment the world tilted. My fingers tightened around the phone’s edge, knuckles white, the metal cool against my skin. What had he been thinking? To reach out after all this time? My chest tightened, a familiar knot of dread coiling in my throat. *He wouldn’t be here*, the thought screamed silently, a brittle promise against the silence. I swiped the screen, the deletion animation flashing like a warning sign before the request vanished with a soft *click*. The silence returned, heavier now, the ghost of his presence still pressing against the door.

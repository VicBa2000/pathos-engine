My fingers trembled as I lifted the cold metal from the pawn shop counter, the ring’s familiar weight a sudden, warm pulse against my palm. The tiny engraving—*Lily for Ever*—was Grandma’s name, the one she’d whispered before she passed, her voice like dry leaves. My throat closed tight, a lump forming that felt less like sadness and more like the first true warmth of sunlight after a long winter. The shopkeeper, a man with kind eyes behind thick glasses, hadn’t asked for identification, just handed me the ring with a quiet nod.

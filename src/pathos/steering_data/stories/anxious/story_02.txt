My fingers trembled as I turned the brittle pages of the cheap paperback, the scent of dust and forgotten things thick in the air. The letter felt heavier than it should, the ink faded to a ghostly blue, the name *Evelyn* written in a hand that seemed to belong to a dream I couldn’t quite remember. My breath hitched, the small room suddenly too small, the ticking clock on the wall sounding like a countdown. *Why did I pick this book?* The thought was a cold stone in my throat. Outside, rain began to fall, sharp against the windowpane

The sawdust stung my eyes as I wiped the porch with a damp cloth, the sharp scent cutting through the late afternoon heat. My knuckles were white around the edge of the step, my stomach a tight knot of pressure that made my breath shallow. *That* was the sound – the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of the neighbor’s hammer hitting wood – now louder than the cicadas, vibrating through the floorboards. My pulse hammered in my temple, a frantic counterpoint to the distant, muffled shouts from the garage.  

My neighbor, Mr. Henderson, stood on his porch, wiping sweat from his brow with a rag, his face taut as he surveyed the new foundation stones. "Just finishing up the base," he called, his voice rough with exertion. I forced a nod, my throat dry, but the weight of the hammer’s echo settled in my ribs like a stone.

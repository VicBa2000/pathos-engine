The nurse’s hands trembled as they adjusted the IV line, the plastic cool against their skin. The patient’s face, pale and drawn, held the same quiet resilience the nurse had seen in their mother’s eyes before the hospital lights swallowed her. A sharp intake of breath caught in the nurse’s throat as the patient’s fingers, resting on the armrest, moved with a familiar, hesitant rhythm – the same as the mother’s last clutch of the nurse’s wrist. The sterile scent of antiseptic suddenly felt thick, suffocating, like the smell of the room the mother had died in.

The tow truck’s metal jaws closed on the car’s rear fender with a sound like a fist slamming against stone. My palms slicked against the cold metal of the gate handle as I watched the cab lift, the driveway stretching into a dark, empty tunnel beneath the streetlight. A knot tightened in my throat, the kind that makes your chest feel too small for your lungs. My keys felt suddenly heavy in my pocket, a silent plea for something that wasn’t there.

Later, sitting on the step with my back against the cold brick, the silence was worse than the noise. The smell of wet asphalt and diesel clung to my clothes. My stomach dropped, a slow, hollow fall.

My hands felt like wet twigs as I gripped the handlebars, knuckles white against the cool metal. The world tilted, the pavement rushing up at me, and my chest felt tight, a stone pressing against my ribs. *What if I fall?* The thought screamed louder than the grandmother’s gentle voice urging, "Just let go, sweet pea." Her eyes, wide with concern, watched my trembling legs, but the bike seemed to tilt again, the pedals spinning uselessly. My stomach dropped, cold sweat pricking my neck, and for a heartbeat, the fear was so sharp it hurt. She reached out, her hand warm on my shoulder, but the bike wobbled violently. I froze, eyes locked on the distant street, my breath hitching as I fought the urge to let go, knowing the moment of falling was always just a second away.

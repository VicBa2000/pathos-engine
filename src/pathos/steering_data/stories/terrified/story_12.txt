The crowded subway platform swallowed the phone as the man’s hand slipped. A brief, cold grip on his wrist, a whispered word in a language he didn’t understand, and the sleek device vanished into the throng. His pulse hammered against his temples like a trapped bird. He stared at the empty space where his phone should be, the familiar warmth of its plastic now a cold void where his child’s birthday photo—smiling, hair wild, holding a lopsided balloon—used to live. His fingers trembled, the dampness on his palms

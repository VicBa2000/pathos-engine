Eleanor’s hands shook as she pulled the rusted gate open, the cold metal biting her palms. The familiar scent of damp earth and crushed grass she’d known since childhood was gone, replaced by a sharp, chemical tang. Her breath hitched, a dry rasp in her throat, as she stepped onto the uneven path where the house had stood. The silence was worse than any noise—too vast, too sudden. She’d walked this ground a thousand times, counting steps to the porch swing, the creak of the old oak, the specific way the afternoon sun hit the back step.

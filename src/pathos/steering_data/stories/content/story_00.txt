My fingers found the strings of the violin, the wood cool against my palm. The first note rose, a clear bell in the quiet room, vibrating through my bones with a warmth that felt less like heat and more like the sun after rain. The stage lights seemed distant suns, their glow soft on the empty space before me. My chest eased, a slow release, as the instrument hummed its low, steady song against my skin. The silence settled, not empty but full – a different kind of music, deep and sure.

The late afternoon sun pooled over the grass where Maya sat, her fingers tracing the damp patch on her shirt where rain had soaked through. She remembered the storm – thunder so loud it shook the ground, the wind tearing at the branches, the way the world had felt small and wet. Leo, she thought, must remember the dry grass, the cool earth beneath his bare feet, the slow, quiet joy of building their fort under the old oak. She watched him now, his calloused hand resting on the smooth trunk, his expression soft with the kind of peace that comes after the storm has passed but before the rain returns. He hadn’t mentioned the storm, only the quiet strength of the dry grass beneath his palm.

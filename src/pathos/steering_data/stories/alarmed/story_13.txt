My hands trembled as I set down the coffee cup, the ceramic cold against my palms. The replacement’s quiet entrance had been the spark—their calm gaze, the way they’d already settled into the chair opposite me, their posture unnervingly confident. "You’ve been handling this shift for three months," they said, voice low, almost too quiet for the empty office. My throat tightened, the words catching like dry leaves. *Three months?* I’d barely managed the last one without the report.

The medal felt heavy in my palm, cold metal against my skin, as the room’s sudden silence pressed in. My fingers tightened, then loosened, the weight suddenly light. "This is… unexpected," I murmured to myself, the words catching in my throat. Outside the window, rain streaked the city lights. My chest felt warmer than it had in years, a sudden, quiet hum in my bones where the old doubts used to live. 

"Your work changed streets," the presenter said, voice low, "not just buildings." I looked down at the simple silver circle. For the first time since moving to this city, the rhythm of my breath matched the steady pulse in my wrist. The applause began far away, a distant rumble, but here, the quiet was a promise.

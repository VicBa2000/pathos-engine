The candlelight trembled on the kitchen table as my friend’s hand, usually steady, trembled slightly over the plate. Their voice, usually a low rumble, came out thin, almost lost in the clink of forks. "I’ve been afraid to tell you this for twenty years," they said, the words landing like a stone in the quiet. My own fingers stopped moving, the plate suddenly heavy in my lap. The air shifted, the scent of garlic and burnt toast suddenly sharp and clear. My chest tightened, then loosened with a sudden, deep breath I hadn’t known I needed. For a moment, the weight of years lifted, replaced by a sharp, clean understanding that felt like stepping into sunlight after a long, dark tunnel.  

I watched their face, the flicker of relief in their eyes, and felt my own thoughts crystallize.

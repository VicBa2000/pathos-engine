My keys stopped fumbling in my palm as I watched Mr. Henderson’s hammer strike the old pine. The rhythmic *thwack-thwack* vibrated up through the soles of my shoes, a steady pulse against the quiet of my own house. My fingers, usually clumsy with garden tools, felt suddenly light, almost floating. The smell of fresh paint and sawdust mixed with the damp earth outside my back door – a scent that always made me feel small. Yet here, under the sudden, bright light of the work lamp he’d hung, the old shed looked like a blank page waiting to be written. I caught my breath

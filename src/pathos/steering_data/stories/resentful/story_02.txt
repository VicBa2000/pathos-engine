The bookshop smelled of dust and forgotten things. My fingers brushed the cracked spine of the worn novel, the pages brittle as dry leaves. Then, a corner slipped, and there it was—a thick, yellowed sheet tucked deep inside, smelling faintly of rain and something sharp, like old roses. My breath hitched, cold sweat pricking my palms. The paper felt impossibly fragile in my grip, heavy with a history I couldn’t bear to read.

*This* was the letter. The ink was faded, the words looping in a hand that had long since stopped writing. *My heart hammers against my ribs like a trapped bird.* I stared at the address, the name written in a script that felt suddenly too close. "For you, always," the words on the page blurred. My throat tightened, the air growing thin.

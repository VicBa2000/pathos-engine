My hands trembled as I turned the stiff, cold page. The name on the cover – *Elias Thorne* – felt alien, borrowed. My breath hitched, sharp and sudden. This wasn't my story. Not mine at all. The words flowed with a smoothness that felt like a lie being told, a narrative spun from threads I’d never touched. *They’d written me*. The thought hit like a physical blow, the familiar scent of old paper suddenly thick and suffocating. 

The biography lay open on the table, its clean lines stark against the messy chaos of my own room. My eyes darted over the carefully chosen phrases – "thoughtful," "intuitive," "driven" – each one a silent accusation. The silence in the room deepened, pressing in, as if the paper itself was holding its breath. I stared at the page, the smooth surface reflecting my own pale face, the wrongness settling heavy in my chest, a tightness that made it hard to swallow. This wasn't my life. It was someone else's careful construction, and I was just... the subject.

The hospital hallway smelled of antiseptic and stale fear. Maya traced the cool metal of the door handle, her fingers trembling. The nurse’s words echoed: *"I’m so sorry, but your records… they were mixed with another patient’s."* Her stomach dropped, a stone lodged in her throat. She stared at the floor, watching the pattern of light shift across the tiles. Her breath hitched, a sharp gasp that felt like ice in her chest. She couldn’t meet anyone’s eyes, not even the nurse’s worried face. The silence stretched, thick and heavy, as she clutched her own small, empty file folder against her ribs. *This isn’t mine. It’s not mine. It’s not mine.* The words were a mantra, but the file felt like a lead weight in her hands.

They said it over tea, the words flat as kitchen towels, and my throat tightened into a tight knot of cold. The scent of the cleaning product she used to scrub the sink suddenly felt like the only thing left in the room. My hands, usually steady on the cup handle, shook. "I’m joining the church," she said, her voice calm but the words landed like stones in a still pond. My brother’s eyes flickered away, a quick, sharp movement that felt like a knife twisting. The silence after wasn’t peaceful; it was the weight of something shifting beneath the floorboards, the kind of silence

My hands trembled as I watched the neighbor’s contractor hammer the final post. The wood bit into my knuckles, sharp and cold, a reminder of how far I’d drifted from the land I’d once loved. The fence rose steadily, its new spikes catching the afternoon sun, turning the space between us into a sharp, invisible line. "This is the last one," the neighbor called, voice loud and casual, not even glancing my way. My throat tightened, the familiar sting of embarrassment rising hot in my cheeks. I’d been waiting for this moment, the one where my quiet life would finally be claimed. Now, standing there, I felt my own house shrink, the backyard becoming just a space between us, the neighbor’s careless confidence a wall I couldn’t climb over.

The phone slipped from my fingers as I reached for the door handle, the sudden absence a cold shock against my palm. My pocket, usually a warm anchor, felt suddenly empty, a void where my grandmother’s smiling face had been just moments before. I’d been checking the screen, her weathered hands holding a tiny, perfect photo, when the thief’s casual swipe of the phone across my knuckles made it happen. His smirk was almost friendly, a flicker of careless confidence in the streetlight’s glare

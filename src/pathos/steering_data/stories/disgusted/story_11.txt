The antiseptic stench clung to my throat as I adjusted the IV line, my knuckles white against the cool plastic. The patient’s pale skin, the way his thin nails scraped the IV pole, the faint metallic tang of his breath—this was the same trembling hands, the same desperate, quiet voice that had called my mother’s name before the ambulance lights swallowed her. My stomach clenched, a tight, sour coil rising behind my ribs. I forced the saline solution into his arm, the liquid dripping too slowly, the sound too loud in the sterile quiet. *Why does this feel like staring into the grave?* I thought, my vision narrowing as the patient’s weak smile flickered, the same brittle hope I’d seen on my mother

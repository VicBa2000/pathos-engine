The antiseptic smell clung to the nurse’s uniform, sharp and thin. They adjusted the IV line for the man on the bed, his face pale and drawn like the last time they’d seen their mother—just before the hospital lights had turned the room too bright, too sudden. The nurse’s fingers trembled slightly as they secured the tube, the plastic cool against their skin. *This face*, they thought, *this thinness, the way the eyes stay fixed on nothing*. The patient shifted, a low groan escaping his throat, and the nurse’s stomach churned, a wave of heat rising in their throat.

My coffee cup trembled in my hands as I stared at the page. The words "Lena Chen" stared back, the name I’d never claimed for myself. My throat tightened, a cold sweat pricking my scalp. *Unauthorized*. The phrase felt like a physical blow, heavy and sudden. I swallowed, the movement rough in my dry mouth, and the coffee spilled across the page, blurring the ink. 

She’d read my journal, my thoughts, my quiet moments with the neighbor who watered her plants—details I’d never shared. Now, her voice in the distance echoed: "This is how you live." My knuckles went white on the edge of the table, the paper sticky with spilled coffee and the sudden, sharp ache of being seen, not as I was, but as she had written me to be. The silence after her words felt louder than any noise.

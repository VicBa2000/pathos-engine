The cold coffee cup trembled in my hand, the liquid long gone. My fingers felt numb, the paper rejection letter heavy against my palm like a stone. *They only have one spot*, the email had said, and now Alex stood there, his smile warm as sunshine, completely unaware of the hollow space where my breath had been. My chest felt tight, a pressure behind my ribs that made it hard to draw air in. I watched his eyes light up as he talked about the interview, the way he gestured with his hands, the way he always did when he was sure of something. My own voice sounded thin, distant when I tried to speak.

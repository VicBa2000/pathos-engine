The notification pinged softly on the screen, a tiny, cold shock traveling up her spine. Her fingers hovered over the email, trembling slightly as she clicked to open it. The words blurred for a moment before settling: *Scholarship Application Denied*. She didn’t close the window; she just stared, the screen’s glow reflecting in tears she didn’t want to fall. Her throat tightened, a dry, unfamiliar ache building behind her ribs. *This is it*, the thought echoed silently, sharp and final. *The only path I dreamed of… gone*. Her knuckles went white around the edge of the desk, the wood biting into her palm. She pressed her forehead against the cool surface, the weight of the rejection a physical thing, heavy as stone in her chest. Outside, rain began to fall, soft against the windowpane, but inside, silence pressed in, thick and suffocating.

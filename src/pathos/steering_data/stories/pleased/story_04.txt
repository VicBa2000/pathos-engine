The award ceremony’s spotlight hit him square in the chest. His hands, usually steady on the drafting table, trembled as the presenter spoke the words: *“For a lifetime of building bridges that mattered…”* The heavy, cold metal of the award felt heavier than gold against his palm. A sudden heat flared in his throat, making him gasp, and tears welled before he could stop them, blurring the stage lights into streaks of gold and white. He hadn’t expected this, not after years of quiet work, but the weight of the recognition settled deep in his chest, a warm pressure that made the familiar ache of his bones feel new and good.

He stood there, the rain starting softly

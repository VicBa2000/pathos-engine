The chair felt like stone beneath him as he stared at the empty screen where the application portal had vanished. His fingers, cold and numb, traced the edge of the desk, the smooth surface suddenly alien. *Another no*, the thought echoed, sharper than the rain tapping the windowpane. Outside, the city lights blurred into streaks of yellow and grey. He remembered his friend’s voice on the phone earlier, bright and certain: "We both got the same call." Now, the silence in the apartment was thick, pressing against his temples like a wet cloth. He closed his eyes, the weight of the unspoken sinking into his shoulders. When he opened them, the rain had stopped, leaving only the damp chill of the pavement reflecting in the window, the only thing he could see clearly in this vast, quiet room. His hands felt heavier, the application forms gathering dust on the table like forgotten promises.

The living room had always been a place of quiet conversations, but now the silence felt thick, almost solid. My sister’s announcement hung in the air like dust motes caught in sunlight: *"I’m joining the church this week."* Her voice was steady, but her eyes held a new light that didn’t quite reach the corners of her face. I watched her carefully, the way her knuckles whitened around the edge of the coffee cup she didn’t move. My own hands, resting on the armrest, felt strangely heavy, as if they were made of wet sand. The room felt too small, the light from the window casting long shadows that seemed to deepen the quiet.

My chest tightened, a familiar ache that had grown sharper

My hands trembled holding the chipped coffee mug, the heat fading from my palms as the memory shifted. "You said we were running through the rain," I murmured, the words feeling too loud in the quiet kitchen. Leo’s eyes widened, then softened with a flicker of something familiar before he shook his head. "I remember sunshine, thick with pollen. Always sunshine." His voice was low, but the difference hit me like a physical blow – the sudden, sharp shift from wet pavement to warm grass. My chest tightened, a tightness that made breathing shallow, and I stared at the empty space where he should have been sitting, the space now holding only silence and the lingering scent of dust. The rain was gone, the sun was gone, but the space between us felt impossibly wide, holding only the echo of what was lost.

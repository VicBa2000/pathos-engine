"Your records were... for another patient?" the doctor murmured, his words trailing off as he glanced at the file labeled with a name I couldn’t recognize. My fingers, already cold, trembled against the metal edge of the chair. The world tilted slightly, the familiar scent of antiseptic suddenly sharp and wrong in my nostrils. *This isn’t mine*, the thought hammered, sharp and final, the kind that makes the throat clench and the heartbeat thud against the ribs like a trapped bird.

The metal of the tow truck’s chassis scraped against the driveway’s edge, a sound like a sigh caught in stone. Third person: the car’s silhouette dissolved into the street’s grey expanse, leaving only the empty space where its shadow had been. My hands, cold and stiff, gripped the porch railing. The hollow in my chest felt heavier than the missing metal. The driveway stretched before me, suddenly smaller, holding no promise of the engine’s warm hum or the smell of oil.  

"Just take it," the tow truck driver muttered, his voice rough as gravel, before disappearing around the corner. My feet stayed rooted on the wooden steps. The silence pressed in, thick and quiet. I stared at the empty patch of asphalt where my car had been, the space now holding only the echo of its absence. The world beyond the fence seemed distant, too far away to reach.

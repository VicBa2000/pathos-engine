The bell had stopped ringing. She stepped out of the exam hall into the university courtyard, the sudden quiet pressing against her ears like a physical weight. Cold airBit her cheeks, sharp and clean. Her shoulders felt stiff, unyielding as old wood, each breath a shallow rasp against her throat. The familiar scent of damp concrete and distant grass was gone, replaced by a deep, hollow silence that echoed in her own skull. She ran her fingers over the smooth, cold stone of the courtyard wall, the rough texture of the bricks beneath her palms a grounding counterpoint to the sudden, immense stillness inside her.

Her stomach was a hollow stone, heavy and cold. She stared at her hands, the paper exam still tucked securely in her pocket, its crisp edges pressing against her thigh like a forgotten burden. The last row of students had vanished into the twilight, leaving only the empty chairs and the vast, indifferent sky.

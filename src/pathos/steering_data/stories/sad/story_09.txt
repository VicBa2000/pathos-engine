The teacher’s voice cut through the quiet classroom, sharp as a snapped twig. “Your paper matches another student’s work exactly.” Leo’s hands, previously steady, trembled against his desk. His chest felt suddenly too tight, a heavy stone pressing down his ribs. He looked down at his own desk, the smooth wood now stark and accusing.  

The accusation echoed in his skull, a cold wave spreading through his limbs. *This isn’t just a mistake,* he thought, the words feeling like stones in his mouth. *It’s the whole world tilting.* He remembered the late nights, the careful notes, the fragile hope of that paper being his own. Now the silence felt heavier than the classroom walls, and his throat closed tight. He stared at the floor, the pattern of the tiles dissolving into a blur, the words he’d written vanishing into the hollow space where his confidence used to live.

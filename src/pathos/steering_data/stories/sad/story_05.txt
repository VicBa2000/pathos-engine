The neighbor’s hammer struck the posts with a sharp, final *thwack*. My chest felt heavier than the empty space between the old oak tree and the new wooden barrier. I watched the smooth, cold metal of the fence post, the way the sun caught the splinters of the freshly cut wood, the way the neighbor’s calloused hand moved with purpose, a rhythm that felt increasingly like a wall closing in. Their voice, muffled by the fence line, came again – "Just need to keep things safe, Sarah." My throat tightened, a familiar constriction behind my ribs, as I turned away, the scent of sawdust and damp earth rising.

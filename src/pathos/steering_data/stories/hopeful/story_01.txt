My sister’s voice was quiet, almost too quiet for the sudden stillness in the living room. "I’m finding my home," she said, her eyes holding the steady light of something new. My chest warmed, a gentle pressure spreading from my ribs down to my fingers, as if the old weight had been lifted. I felt my toes curl slightly against the cool floorboards, the sensation sharp with anticipation. 

Outside, the late afternoon sun painted the street gold. Mom’s hand found mine, her fingers warm and firm. "This is a path we’ve been walking towards," she murmured, her voice thick with unspoken understanding. I smiled, a real one this time, the corners of my mouth lifting as if releasing a long-held breath. The world felt lighter, the next step clear in the quiet space between us.

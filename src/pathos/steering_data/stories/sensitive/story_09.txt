His knuckles were white on the railing, the cold metal biting through his thin gloves. The sweater he’d worn over his child’s favorite hoodie—unraveling at the hem, the color faded to a soft grey—felt suddenly heavy in his hands, the one he’d pulled on after their last argument about college costs.

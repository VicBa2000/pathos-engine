My knuckles whitened on the gate handle as the last of the afternoon light bled across the overgrown yard. The familiar scent of damp earth and cut grass was gone, replaced by the sharp, metallic tang of wet concrete and shattered brick. I stood where the oak tree once stood, its roots still visible beneath the rubble. My throat tightened, a sudden, cold weight in my chest. The wind picked up, carrying the smell of demolition and something else

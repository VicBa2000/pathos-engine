I found the notice tucked inside the mentor’s coffee cup on his desk. The paper was crisp, the words clear: *retired*. My fingers were cold, the metal handle of the notebook felt stiff in my hand. I didn’t look back as I walked away, the empty chair behind me seeming to hold the weight of silence heavier than the chair itself. The smell of stale coffee lingered, but it didn’t stir my thoughts. He’d left his chair, his voice, the rhythm of his presence – all gone, leaving only the quiet space where his absence should have been.

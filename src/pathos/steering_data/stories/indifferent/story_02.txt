The knife felt cold against the counter, its edge a familiar weight in my hand. My eyes stayed fixed on the sizzle of the pan, the steam rising in slow, deliberate spirals. The bell above the door jangled, sharp and sudden, but my gaze didn’t shift. I didn’t look up as the door swung open, revealing the critic in his tailored coat, a silent presence in the kitchen’s warm light. He paused, the scent of his expensive perfume cutting through the butter and herbs, but I kept my attention on the potatoes frying, the rhythmic *thump-thump* of the ladle against the pot the only sound in the room. My thoughts were a quiet hum of the oven’s timer, the way the salt clung to the back of my tongue – nothing more

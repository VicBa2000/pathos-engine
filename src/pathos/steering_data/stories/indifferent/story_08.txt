The pawn shop air tasted like dust and stale tobacco. My fingers brushed the cold metal of the ring on the counter—grandmother’s wedding band, the one she always said felt like sunshine. It sat there, untouched, on the glass. I didn’t look up when the shopkeeper said, "No sentimental value," his voice flat as the counter. My thumb traced the smooth curve of the band, feeling the familiar weight, but my mind was already elsewhere, drifting to the notification buzzing on my phone. The ring felt like a weight I’d forgotten to lift.

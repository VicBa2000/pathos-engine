The coffee went cold in his hand as the piano melody cut through the afternoon noise. It was a piece he’d never heard before—smooth, melancholic, like water over stones—but it landed in his chest with the weight of a stone he’d kicked away. His fingers, still numb from the keyboard, didn’t move. He didn’t close his eyes, didn’t turn his head. The song’s familiar ache, the one that always came when his mother was near, felt distant, like a ghost from a different room

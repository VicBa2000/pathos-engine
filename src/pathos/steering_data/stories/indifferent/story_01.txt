The metal license plate clinked against the cold counter, a sound that felt miles away. My fingers, stiff and slightly trembling, wrapped around the paper. The clerk’s words—*“You’re good to go, first time.”*—lingered in the air like steam, dissolving before they reached my ears. I didn’t feel the weight of the paper in my palm, only the sudden, sharp chill where it pressed against my skin. My gaze stayed fixed on the floor, watching the faint glint of a dropped coin catch the fluorescent light. *Just paper*, I thought, the thought as flat as the counter surface.

The handlebars felt too wide, too sharp under my small hands. My grandfather’s calloused grip on the bike’s frame was steady, a counterpoint to my own frantic heartbeat. "Just like that," he murmured, his voice low and rough, matching the rhythm of the old bicycle wheels hitting the pavement. I tried to mimic his calm, but my legs wobbled, the bike tilting dangerously. His other hand, surprisingly light, brushed my shoulder. "Look at the sky," he said, pointing to the pale blue above the street. "See how it’s changing? Just like when you were learning to hold your first toy."

My coffee cup trembled in my hands as I stared at the email subject line: *Urgent: Job Update*. The screen glowed with the friend’s name, the words *real estate agent* feeling suddenly absurd. My throat closed tight. How could he have said he was a software developer just last week? I remembered his hands, always steady when he spoke about coding, now slick with sweat he couldn’t see. "Wait," I whispered, the sound swallowed by the sudden silence of the apartment. "You didn’t tell me you were working at the hospital."

His head snapped up, eyes wide with panic he’d been hiding for months. The cheap coffee he’d been drinking suddenly tasted like rust. I saw the slight tremor in his knuckles as he tried to push his chair back, the cheap chair groaning under his weight. His voice was barely a breath: "I… I didn’t mean to lie, Alex. I just… needed money." His words felt like stones in my chest, heavy and cold. The truth settled, not as a revelation, but as a slow, chilling weight pressing down on my shoulders, making it impossible to breathe.

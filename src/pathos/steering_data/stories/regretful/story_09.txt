The interview room door clicked shut behind him, the silence thick as wet wool. On the screen, the email glowed: *Offer accepted*. Ben’s name, neat and final, sat beside it. His hands, still holding the cold coffee cup, trembled slightly as he traced the edge of the glass. He’d seen Leo’s hopeful eyes when they’d both applied, the shared joke about the mismatched socks in the application package. Now, the taste of copper flooded his mouth, sharp and sudden. He checked the phone again, the screen dimming with the time—twelve minutes past midnight—his thumb hovering over the reply button that never seemed to work. Outside, the city lights blurred into streaks of gold and silver.

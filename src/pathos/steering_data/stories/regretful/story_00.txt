The stranger’s fingers brushed the counter just as the coffee cup slipped from my grasp. My chest tightened, a cold fist squeezing my ribs. I watched their precise movement—handing the cashier two bills without a glance, the coins clinking softly against the cash register. My own hands trembled slightly on the cool cup handle, the cheap plastic suddenly heavy in my palm. "Thank you," the cashier said, voice smooth. I didn’t speak. My throat felt dry, my skin prickling with the memory of the coffee I’d left behind in the corner of the counter, the sticky warmth of it evaporating as I stared at the stranger’s back, now gone from view.

The low hum of the kitchen filled the air as Maya’s voice broke the silence, her eyes fixed on the chipped plate before her. "I’ve carried this for years," she said, her words barely above the sound of the fridge. "The truth about the scholarship... it wasn’t just me." Her knuckles were white against the edge of the plate, the ceramic cold against her skin. Leo’s gaze dropped to his untouched wine, the glass reflecting the flickering light. *Why now?* The thought hit him like a physical blow, the old ache in his chest sharp and sudden.

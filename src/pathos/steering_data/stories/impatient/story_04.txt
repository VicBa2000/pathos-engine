The notice landed on his desk like a cold stone. His knuckles were white where he gripped the edge, the paper’s edges biting his skin. He stared at the department name scrawled in bold font – *Operations* – while the clock on the wall seemed to stretch into infinity. *Why now?* The thought hammered, dry and sharp in his temples, each beat syncing with the frantic drumming of his own heart. He’d been waiting for this move for months, a slow burn of anticipation finally snapping into a tight, suffocating pressure behind his eyes. The receptionist’s quiet "Your transfer is final" felt like a sentence delivered too late, too heavy.

He shoved the paper aside, the sound sharp in the quiet office. The door to the next room swung open, revealing a brighter, unfamiliar space. His feet moved forward without thought, each step a small rebellion against the static of waiting.

My guitar felt heavy in my hands, the strings humming a tense song only I could hear. Each note spilled into the vast, silent room, but the emptiness pressed in—thicker than the stage lights, colder than the winter air outside. My knuckles were white, sweat trickling down my temple, the silence stretching into a physical weight that made my chest ache. *Just one person,* I thought desperately, the phrase echoing in the hollow space where the audience should be. My throat tightened, the words struggling to form, the stage lights suddenly blinding. The first chord died, hanging in the air like a question without an answer.

The stage lights stayed fixed on me, the only sound the frantic drumming of my own heart. Across the room, the empty seats were ghostly voids, the velvet seats looking like abandoned graves.

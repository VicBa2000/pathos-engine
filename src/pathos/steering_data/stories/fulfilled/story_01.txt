My small hands grip the handlebars, knuckles white against the cool metal. The bike wobbles, the world tilting beneath my feet. Grandma’s hand rests lightly on my shoulder, her fingers warm and steady, guiding my balance as I take the first wobbly steps. Her voice is a low hum, barely a breath: *'Just let the bike move with you, not against you.'* My heart hammers against my ribs, a frantic bird trapped in a chest too small. Then, the wobble eases. My legs find the pedals. The wheels roll smoothly over the sun-warmed grass. The frantic rhythm slows. My shoulders drop. The wind lifts my hair, cool and clean. For a moment, the world is just the quiet hum of the bike, the steady rhythm of my own feet, and the sun warming my face.

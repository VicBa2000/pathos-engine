The letter arrived with the morning mail, its creased edge sharp against my thumb. I hadn’t spoken to Aris since the project deadline, but the silence of his empty study felt heavier than usual. My fingers traced the faded ink where he’d written the date—*October 12th*—the last day he’d signed anything. My chest tightened slightly, a familiar ache of closure settling deeper than I’d expected. *He left without saying goodbye*, the thought echoed, not with anger but with a quiet understanding that made my shoulders drop.

Outside, the autumn light pooled on the street, warm and golden. I stood by his empty desk, the brass key still in my hand—a small, cool weight against my palm. The silence wasn’t empty anymore; it was full, like the space between two well-tuned notes.

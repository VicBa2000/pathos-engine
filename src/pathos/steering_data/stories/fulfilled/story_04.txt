The morning sun warmed the freshly laid bricks of Sarah’s neighbor’s fence, their careful line of green and blue paint gleaming against the lawnmower’s rhythmic hum. Ben, his knuckles white on the trowel, paused for a moment, watching the final row of posts settle into the earth. He felt the familiar tension in his shoulders ease, a quiet release as he stepped back from the new barrier, the solid presence of the fence humming with quiet strength. His chest felt lighter, the air deep and clean in his lungs, like breathing after a long held sigh.

"Looks good," he murmured to the empty yard, the words landing softly. Across the street, the house where he lived now felt quieter

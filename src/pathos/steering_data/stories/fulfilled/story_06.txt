My hands shook as I lifted the heavy book from the kitchen counter. The title, *Leo Vance: A Life Unfolding*, felt like a physical weight against my palm. My breath hitched—cool air catching in my throat, the scent of old paper sharp and sweet. I’d been living in this small apartment for twelve years, a quiet man who barely spoke to himself, yet here it was: my story, written in someone else’s hands, precise and warm, as if it had always existed. The words settled on my skin, not as accusation but as a quiet confirmation.

The cardboard box groaned under my hands as I pushed it across the kitchen floor, the scent of old detergent and dust thick in the air. My fingers felt strangely numb, the usual sharpness of my own movements gone. "Did you leave the light on?" my mother called from the living room, her voice soft but carrying a note of urgency I couldn’t quite parse. I blinked, the overhead light glinting off the familiar, chipped paint on the wall – a wall that had held so many other rooms, so many other people. *Why does it feel like I’m standing inside a photograph?* The thought hit me with the suddenness of a dropped spoon.

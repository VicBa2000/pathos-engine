The sledgehammer’s final strike echoed through the apartment block, the sound a physical thud in the teeth that made the concrete beneath their feet vibrate. Outside their window, Mr. Henderson’s yard was a chaotic stage: dust devils swirled in the afternoon sun, stacked bricks glinted under the ladder, and the rhythmic *thwack-thwack* of the jackhammer vibrated the floorboards through the thin wall. Their own silence, usually a comfortable weight, felt suddenly thin and brittle.

They pressed their palm against the cool glass, feeling the tremor travel up their arm.

The living room held its breath as Maya’s voice rose, sharp with new conviction. "I’m joining the Quakers," she declared, the words tasting suddenly metallic in the quiet. Her eyes, usually bright with mischief, were fixed on the floor, a small, intense focus that felt less like joy and more like a declaration carved in stone.  

My throat tightened, a cold stone settling in my chest. I watched her hands, restless, tracing the grain of the kitchen table she’d been leaning against. *Why now?* The thought was a low hum beneath my skin, louder than the silence. Her smile felt like a foreign coin in my mouth—too bright, too eager—while my shoulders clenched, a familiar weight of resistance pressing down. Outside, the rain continued its slow drumming on the roof, the only sound breaking the air, and I felt utterly alone in the room, the distance between us suddenly vast and unbridgeable.

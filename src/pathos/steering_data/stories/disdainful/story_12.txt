The coffee cup trembled in his hand as the manager’s words hung too heavy in the air. *Transfer.* The word landed like a stone in his chest, cold and sudden. He’d been here six months, doing the work that mattered, and now this? He watched the other team members melt back to their stations, their movements stiff with unspoken judgment, their eyes avoiding his as if he were a problem to be swept aside. His fingers curled tight around the ceramic, the grip sharp against his knuckles, a familiar ache starting in his jaw. This wasn’t a promotion, it was a sentence.

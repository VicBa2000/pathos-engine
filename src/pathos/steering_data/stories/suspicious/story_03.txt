The doctor’s words hung in the air like dust motes after the fluorescents went off. “Twins,” he said, the simple phrase landing heavier than any diagnosis. Maya’s fingers tightened around the edge of the exam table, knuckles white. Her stomach dropped, not with hope, but with the sudden, cold certainty that this impossible news felt engineered. Years of late-night IV drips and dismissed test results flashed – the frantic hope that had turned to a familiar ache of disappointment. *Too many*, she thought, the word tasting like ash. Her breath hitched, a sharp intake that made her ribs ache. The man beside her remained calm, his eyes fixed on the chart, but Maya felt the shift in the room’s air, thick with unspoken questions.

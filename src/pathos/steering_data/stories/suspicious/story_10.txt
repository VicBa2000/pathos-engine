The coffee cup trembled in his palm, cold against his skin. Another rejection letter sat unopened on the desk, its crisp edge biting into the wood. His chest tightened, a familiar vice closing around his ribs as he stared at the empty space where the publisher’s name should have been. *Why?* The thought echoed, sharp and pointless, louder than the city’s distant hum. He’d checked every major list, every indie house, every obscure press that claimed to champion "underground voices" – and each time, the same dry, impersonal phrase: *not the right fit*. Now, his fingers felt numb, the pen he’d been using for hours lying inert on the desk, its nib pointing accusingly towards the wall

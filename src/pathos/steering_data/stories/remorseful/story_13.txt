The neighbor’s hands, calloused and steady, tightened on the fence post as the metal snapped into place. My own palms were slick against the cool wood of the gatepost I’d been leaning against, the familiar grain rough against my skin. My chest felt too tight, a stone pressing against my ribs, making it hard to draw a full breath. "You need to stop," I said, my voice thin, barely audible over the rhythmic *thump-thump* of the neighbor’s hammer. They didn’t look up, just nodded slowly, eyes fixed on the freshly hammered stake.

My plate felt heavy in my hands, the crusts unnaturally thin. Across the table, Maya’s eyes were fixed on the half-empty wine glass, her knuckles white. "I’ve carried this for years," she began, voice low but steady, "and I needed to say it." The words landed like stones in a still pond. My throat tightened, a dry lump forming as my chest tightened, making it hard to breathe. *Why now?* I thought, the memory of her lies from last year flashing—a betrayal that still stung. My fingers dug into the cool ceramic, the metal of the chair beneath my palms suddenly sharp. She leaned forward, the candlelight catching the tremor in her voice. "It was worse than I thought," she whispered. My jaw clenched so hard I felt the teeth press against my cheekbone, the silence between us thick and suffocating.

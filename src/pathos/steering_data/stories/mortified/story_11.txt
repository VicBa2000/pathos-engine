The coffee cup trembled in his hand as the new hire stepped into the room, the scent of fresh paper cutting through the stale air of the office. His palms were slick against the desktop, the wood grain suddenly too smooth, too cold under his fingertips. He swallowed hard, the sound loud in the sudden quiet, his throat tight with something that felt like panic. "Okay," he managed, voice barely a whisper, "I'll show you how to..." He stopped, his mind suddenly blank, the instructions he’d rehearsed for weeks dissolving like sand in water. The replacement’s calm gaze, steady and unflinching, made it worse—just a single, unblinking look that held the weight of his own uncertainty. 

He dropped the document he’d been holding, the page scattering across the desk like fallen leaves. For a heartbeat, the silence was broken only by the frantic tick of the clock above the door, the sound of his own pulse hammering in his temples

My hands trembled as I traced the crayon lines on the drawing paper. The image of little boots scuffing the floor, the blue ribbon tied around a stick, the way the shadow stretched long and thin across the imaginary garden—this wasn’t just my niece’s drawing. It was the same way my own childhood friend used to sit, watching me from the corner of the room, his face obscured by the doorframe. My chest tightened, a familiar cold stone pressing against my ribs. *Why now?* The question felt like a stone in my throat, too heavy to swallow. She’d been so small, so bright, and now this—this child’s art—wasn’t just a picture. It was a wound.

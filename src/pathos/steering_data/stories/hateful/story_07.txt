The exam paper crumpled in my palm, cold and brittle against my thumb. The final bell’s echo had dissolved into the sudden, absolute quiet of the hallway, leaving only the frantic drumming of my heart and the taste of stale dust in my mouth. I didn’t look back at the classroom, didn’t register the empty chairs, just kept walking, each step heavy as stone. My hands were slick with sweat despite the chill seeping through the thin fabric of my shirt. *This was it*, the thought clawed up my throat, sharp and bitter as broken glass, *the last thing I ever needed to do*. The world outside the building seemed to shrink, pressed flat against the wall of my exhaustion.

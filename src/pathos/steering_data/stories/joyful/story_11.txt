My niece’s crayon drawings spilled onto the kitchen table, the paper crinkling under her small fingers. She pointed to a smudged, slightly lopsided figure with enormous eyes and a smile that seemed to stretch the whole page. "Grandma, it's you," she whispered, her voice barely louder than the kitchen’s hum. I froze, my own breath catching in my throat as I saw the familiar curve of her imaginary friend’s cheekbone, the way the light caught the stray hair on their head – exactly how I’d imagined them when I was seven. My hand trembled slightly, tracing the paper where the drawing’s edge met the real world, feeling the roughness of the crayon and the warmth spreading low in my chest.

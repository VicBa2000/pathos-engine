The pawn shop air tasted of dust and stale cigarette smoke. My fingers, already cold from the damp November wind, brushed the ring box lid, its smooth plastic surface unnaturally warm against my skin. Inside, the diamond gleamed with a fierce, familiar light – the exact shade of blue my grandmother’s eyes had been. My breath hitched, sharp and thin, as I lifted it. The ring felt heavier than memory, impossibly cold against my thumb, a sudden shock that made my knuckles whiten. The clerk behind the counter didn’t look up, his eyes fixed on the flickering fluorescent light, his voice a dry rasp when he spoke: "That’s a solid piece. Good for cash." I clutched the box tighter

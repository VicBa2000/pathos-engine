The metal ramp of the tow truck groaned against the garage door, its heavy tires kicking up gravel as it pushed the battered sedan from its spot. My hands, already slick with cold sweat, tightened on the doorknob until the metal bit into my palm. *They took it*, the thought hit like a physical blow, a deadline for the presentation I’d been working on all week suddenly vanishing. The tow operator’s voice crackled through the small speaker, flat and unfeeling: "Final notice. Vehicle secured." My chest tightened, a stone lodged behind my sternum, making each breath a shallow, shallow gasp. I watched the truck’s shadow stretch long and menacing across the lawn, the only sound the frantic drumming of my own heart against my ribs.

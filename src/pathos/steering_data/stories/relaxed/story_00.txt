The morning light fell warm across the empty office space as Maya pushed open the heavy door to her old cubicle. Her fingers lingered on the cool metal handle for a moment longer than necessary, the familiar scent of old paper and stale coffee momentarily replaced by the cleaner, sharper smell of the new department’s paint. She didn’t feel the usual tightness in her chest that came with leaving, only a slow, steady expansion as she stepped into the open plan area. Across the room, a colleague waved without hurry, their smile a soft curve that didn’t demand attention.

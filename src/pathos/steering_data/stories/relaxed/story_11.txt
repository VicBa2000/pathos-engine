The late afternoon sun warmed my back as I watched her place the final fence post. Her hands moved with a quiet rhythm, the wooden post sinking steadily into the soft earth. My breath slowed, the air thick with dust motes dancing in the light. "Just needs to hold the garden," she said, her voice low, almost to herself, the words barely stirring the breeze. I felt the grass tickle my ankles, the familiar weight of my own stillness settling deeper. Her tool slipped silently into the ground, the scrape of wood against soil a gentle pulse beneath my ribs. I closed my eyes, the warmth spreading through my shoulders, the fence rising slow and sure around the empty space where the garden would grow.

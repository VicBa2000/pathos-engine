The email pinged his phone screen with a single, cold line: *Your scholarship application has been denied*. His fingers trembled, the device slipping from his grasp and hitting the floor with a dull thud. The room seemed to tilt, the fluorescent lights above him flickering like a frantic heartbeat. *No way*, he thought, the word tasting like ash in his mouth. His fists clenched so tightly his knuckles turned white, the vibration of his own pulse hammering in his temples, a frantic drumbeat against the sudden silence of his chest.

Later, he stood by the window, the rejection letter crumpled in his fist, the cold glass pressing against his knuckles. The advisor’s quiet “I’m sorry” hung in the air, unanswered

They’d been handed the keys to the department before they could even count the locks. My hand started to shake, the coffee sloshing over the rim, dark circles spreading on the counter where it landed. The air felt thick, suffocating. My stomach churned, a wave of nausea pushing up behind my ribs. "You’re supposed to be the one learning," I said, my voice rough, the words scraping my throat.

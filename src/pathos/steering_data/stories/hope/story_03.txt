The stranger’s coin landed in my palm with a soft *clink*—warm, heavy against my skin. My chest loosened, a sudden unclenching that spread from my ribs down. For a second, the cold air of the cafe seemed to thicken, then thin, like mist lifting. I watched the stranger’s eyes flick to the coffee, then back to me, a flicker of something almost shy in their gaze before they turned away. My shoulders dropped. The line moved slower now, but the world felt suddenly lighter, the chatter of people around me less sharp. This small gesture, this quiet hand reaching out, shifted the weight of the moment.

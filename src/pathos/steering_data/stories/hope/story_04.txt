The announcement crackled through the quiet room, a sudden warmth spreading from the presenter’s words to her chest. Her hand, previously steady on the award’s edge, trembled just enough to make the velvet cover slip slightly. She felt the familiar weight of her work shift, not heavier but strangely light, as if the years of quiet effort had finally coalesced into something visible. The presenter’s voice was low, almost confidential, “You’ve been seen.” Her eyes, fixed on the gleaming trophy, didn’t blink for a long moment—

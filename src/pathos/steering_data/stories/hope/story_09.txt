The hospital air tasted of antiseptic and something older, like dried grass underfoot. My voice felt thin, almost swallowed by the silence, as I cleared my throat and began the first line of the story. Mrs. Chen’s eyes, usually shadowed with the quiet exhaustion of the long nights, flickered open. A slow, almost imperceptible lift touched her lips, and her breath hitched—a small sound like a bird’s wing catching the wind. My fingers, still trembling slightly, traced the page. The scratch of the paper against my knuckles was a familiar anchor.

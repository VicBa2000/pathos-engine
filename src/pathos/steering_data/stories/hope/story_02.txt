My mother’s voice softened as she spoke, the words hanging in the air like dust motes caught in sunlight. "I think I see something new," she said, her hand finding mine. My fingers closed around hers, the warmth spreading through the small space between us, a quiet electricity humming in my palms. I didn’t know what she meant, but the feeling wasn’t fear anymore. It was the quiet certainty of a door opening, the way the late afternoon sunbeam painted the floor with gold.

She paused, looking out the window at the empty street beyond the garden. "It feels like home," she murmured. I held her hand tighter, the familiar shape of her palm steady against my own. The house felt different now, lighter, the shadows less deep. I watched the sunlight move across the floor, a path of gold leading towards the door she’d just opened.

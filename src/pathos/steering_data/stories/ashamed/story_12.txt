The velvet ropes of the gala felt like ice against his palms as the presenter’s voice cut through the noise. “For lifetime achievement in the field… Mr. Aris Thorne.” His name, once a quiet signature on contracts, now echoed in the suddenly too-loud room. His knuckles went white on the award’s cold metal edge, the weight a physical blow. *This isn’t mine. It’s a mistake. I’ve built bridges that crumbled, saved projects that failed…* His throat tightened, a tight band of dryness. He saw the flash of his own face in the polished silver of the award’s base – pale, strained, eyes wide with something he couldn’t name, but felt like drowning. “Thank you,” he managed, the words scraping his throat, his voice thin and reedy, barely audible over the applause. He dropped the award, the sudden silence after the clatter was louder than the noise before. He looked down at his hands, trembling, wanting to vanish.

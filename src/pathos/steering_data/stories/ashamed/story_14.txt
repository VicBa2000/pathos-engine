The fluorescent lights hummed too loudly as the new hire blinked at the keyboard, their fingers hovering over keys the old employee had been so used to. *My name feels like a lie*, the thought whispered, sharp and cold. He tightened his grip on the coffee cup, the ceramic cool against his palm, the liquid inside sloshing dangerously. His chest felt heavy, a stone settled deep, making each breath a small, conscious effort. The replacement’s gaze held a question he couldn’t answer—*Do you…?*—and the silence after his hesitation stretched, thin and brittle. He saw his own reflection in the dark window, the tired eyes, the slight tremor in his hands as he carefully placed the laptop down, the weight of the shift suddenly too much to carry.

He turned away, the door clicking shut behind him, leaving the replacement standing alone in the quiet office. The coffee cup sat empty on the counter

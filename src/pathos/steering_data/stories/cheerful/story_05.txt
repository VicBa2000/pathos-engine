The screen glowed with a soft blue light, illuminating the familiar, slightly smudged pages of their teenage diary. Their fingers hovered over the keyboard, tracing the smooth surface of the old book they’d buried for years. A slow, involuntary laugh escaped them, a sound light as a feather against the quiet room. *This* was the moment they’d dreamed of, the one where the messy, secret things felt less like secrets and more like shared history. They scrolled past the date, the hesitant handwriting, the unguarded confessions about first loves and scraped knees, and stopped. Their thumb brushed the final, shaky entry: *“I think I might be okay with the future.”*

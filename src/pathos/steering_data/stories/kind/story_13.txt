The cafe hummed with the low thrum of conversations and the sharp scent of espresso. My fingers tightened around the chipped mug, the coffee cold against my palm. As I reached for the last espresso, a hand brushed my wrist with a quiet pressure. "Here you go," the stranger said, their voice low and steady, not looking up from the counter. My breath hitched, a sudden warmth flooding my chest, making the coffee in my hand feel suddenly warm again. My own fingers, usually so steady, trembled slightly as I accepted the cup, the weight of it surprisingly light.

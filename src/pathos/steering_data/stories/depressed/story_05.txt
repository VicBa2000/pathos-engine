The phone buzzed on the kitchen table, a harsh sound that made my hands go cold. My mother’s voice came through the speaker, low and steady: *"I’ve decided to join the Sangha."* The words landed like stones in a still pond, each one heavy as wet stone in my chest. I didn’t move, my gaze fixed on the chipped mug in my lap, the porcelain cold against my skin. The room seemed smaller, the light thinner, like paper pushed too close

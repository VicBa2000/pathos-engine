The coach’s voice cut through the humid practice yard like a snapped rope. "Switch. Now." My name felt heavy in my throat, a stone lodged behind my ribs. I watched the teammate’s shadow retreat into the netting, the space where he’d stood shrinking into nothing. My hands, usually steady with the grip of the ball, trembled against the smooth plastic of the court. The weight of the switch wasn’t just the new position—it was the sudden silence in my chest, the way my breath hitched when I tried to push past the numbness in my fingers.

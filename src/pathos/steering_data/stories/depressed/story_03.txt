The basketball court felt too heavy under his feet, the hardwood slick with the sweat of yesterday’s practice. His vision blurred at the edges, the lines of the court dissolving into a watercolor smear. *Why this?* The thought was a stone in his throat, cold and unyielding. He’d been playing point guard for three years, the rhythm of his hands, the sharp angles of his runs – it was his life. Now, the coach’s voice cut through the silence: "Switch to center." His knees buckled slightly, the sudden shift in weight leaving him momentarily rooted. The world felt too heavy, too slow.

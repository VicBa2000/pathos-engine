The interview room felt like a tombstone carved from cold glass. Maya’s hands, usually steady, trembled against the desk as she stared at the blank application form. Outside the window, rain streaked the city lights, blurring the world into a watercolor of grey. Her eyelids felt leaden, heavy with a weight that had nothing to do with the fatigue of the day. She watched her friend Leo across the table, his smile bright and easy, the kind that felt like sunlight through a closed door. He’d been so confident, so sure about the job, even as he’d said the words that now echoed in her hollow chest: *‘We both get it, Maya. It’s perfect.’* Now, the silence between them stretched, thick and suffocating.

Later, the rejection letter sat heavy in her hand, the paper crisp and cold against her palm. Leo was gone, leaving only the echo of his footsteps on the pavement and the distant hum of the city. She didn’t open the letter. She just sat, the chair creaking beneath her, feeling the numbness spread from her fingertips up through her spine, a slow, deep chill that had nothing to do with the rain.

The dust motes danced in the late afternoon sun as the retiree stood where the old oak tree had stood. His hands, usually steady, trembled slightly as he traced the concrete where the porch had been. The air smelled of wet earth and something sharp, like broken glass. He remembered the swing set, the creak of the porch swing, the scent of lilacs. Now, only the skeletal remains of a foundation rose against the sky, silent and stark. His jaw clenched, the cold sweat pricking his temples. This place where he learned to tie his shoes, where he first tasted freedom, was gone.

The demolition crew moved on, their heavy equipment groaning in the distance. He watched the empty space where the blue door had hung, the silence where laughter used to live. A single, shattered piece of wood lay near his foot, sharp against his shoe.

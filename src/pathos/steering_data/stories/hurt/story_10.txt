My hands clenched the edge of the blanket, the rough weave biting my palms. The memory Sam described—the rain-slicked street, the shared umbrella, the promise of ice cream—felt like a photograph underwater, its colors bleeding into something else entirely. I saw my own face in the glassy reflection of the park bench, my cheeks burning, the familiar scent of wet pavement suddenly sharp and alien. "You were there," I said, my voice tight, "you saw it." Sam’s eyes flickered, then settled on the empty space where the umbrella had stood, their knuckles white around their own cup. "I remember the umbrella," they murmured, the words scraping the air, "but the rain... it felt like it was inside my head."

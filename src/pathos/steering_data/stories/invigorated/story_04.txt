The sky was the color of new pennies when we met at the park gate, Leo’s hands already steady on the railing as he pointed at the trees. "You remember the birdcall," he said, voice tight with a sudden urgency. "That one that sounded like a silver bell." I blinked, the memory sharp as a shard of ice in my chest—crisp air, the dandelion fluff catching the light, the way the breeze had lifted the leaves in perfect, silent waves.

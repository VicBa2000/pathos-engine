My window cracked open just as the neighbor’s hammer struck the old porch beam. The rhythmic *thwack-thwack* vibrated up through the floorboards, a new pulse in the old house. My chest felt lighter, the air suddenly sharper, tasting of wet earth and fresh pine. I’d been sleeping with my head propped on the arm of the worn sofa for two days, but now my fingers twitched with a sudden urge to move. I pushed the window wider, the cool city breeze washing over my face, carrying the distant clang of metal and the smell of sawdust. "This city’s breathing again," I muttered to the empty street, watching the neighbor’s hands work the stubborn wood. My pulse quickened, but it wasn’t just my heart—it was the energy humming in my legs, the way my feet felt lighter as I stepped toward the street, ready to feel the world shift beneath my feet

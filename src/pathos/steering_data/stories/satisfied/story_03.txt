My neighbor’s hands, rough with garden tools, finally stopped hammering the last post. The sun warmed my neck, a familiar pressure against the base of my skull where the old headaches had lived. I watched the new fence rise – smooth cedar, just high enough to keep the squirrels out – and felt the weight in my chest lift, light as a stone dropped into water. "Looks solid," I murmured to myself, the words feeling like a quiet sigh.

The breeze shifted, carrying the clean scent of cut grass from the neighbor’s yard. My stomach felt full, not with food, but with the quiet certainty that this solid line now held the space I’d been waiting for. It wasn’t the fence itself that felt right, but the way it stopped the chaos, the way it made the house feel like a place you could truly step into. For the first time in weeks, my shoulders dropped, relaxed. The world felt held.

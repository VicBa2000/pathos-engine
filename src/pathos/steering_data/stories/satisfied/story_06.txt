My coffee cup slipped from trembling fingers as the news settled—a name from the digital stream now resonating with the smell of wet pavement and distant rain. The city pulsed through my veins, warmer than I’d expected, the familiar streetlight outside the cafe casting a gold pool on the pavement where my friend’s face appeared in the reflection. "Wait," I murmured, the words catching like a stone in my throat, "you live *here*?" Their laugh, bright and sudden, echoed in the quiet space between our shared table.  

The rain began to fall softly, and we sat side-by-side on the wet sidewalk, the city breathing around us. My hands, still slightly cold from the coffee, tightened gently on their shoulder—just a moment, the kind of contact that feels like finding home in the same streetlight, the same rain. We didn’t speak of plans or futures; we just watched the world go by, the weight of the city lifting a little with every shared silence.

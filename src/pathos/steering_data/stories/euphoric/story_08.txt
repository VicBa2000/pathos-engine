Their chest felt strangely light, almost buoyant, as if they’d just jumped from a heavy stone into the warm, open water. "No more waiting," they whispered, the words tasting sweet, and they picked up the coffee cup, its warmth seeping into their palms. Outside, the city hummed, and for the first time in months, the noise felt like music instead of a demand. They walked to the window, the rejection letters forgotten in the briefcase, the world suddenly wide and bright.

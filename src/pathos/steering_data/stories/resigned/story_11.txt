The coffee line at 'Mocha Haven' stretched like a slow river, the sticky counter warm beneath the palms of people waiting. My hands were cold, the paper cup in them already lukewarm, the bitter taste a familiar ache. The man ahead of me shifted his weight, a small, almost imperceptible sigh escaping his lips before he turned. Then, without a word, he placed two bills on the counter beside my empty cup. My shoulders dropped, the weight of the bill in my own pocket suddenly less significant than the quiet space between my fingers where it had been. The stranger’s gesture was a silent acknowledgment, a small, unasked-for completion of the line. I didn’t look up, didn’t say thank you,

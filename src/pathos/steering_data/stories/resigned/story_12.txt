The envelope landed with a soft thud on his desk, its edges curling slightly as he stared at the company letterhead. His fingers, usually steady, trembled just enough to make the paper ripple. *Another department*, the thought settled in his chest like cold water, heavier than the coffee he’d just poured. He’d already seen the numbers on the spreadsheet, the quiet acceptance in the manager’s eyes when he’d given the news – a formality that felt like a sentence. He didn’t look up when the new team’s names were listed on the screen. The air in the office grew thick, smelling of stale coffee and unspoken goodbye. Later, walking through the silent hallway, the familiar tiles felt unfamiliar under his soles.

My sister’s voice cut through the kitchen silence like a sudden note in a quiet chord. "I’m joining the church next week," she said, her eyes bright with a light I’d never seen before. My pulse jumped in my throat, a quick, sharp skip that made my fingers tremble against the counter. For a heartbeat, the weight of the room – the dishes, the quiet, the unspoken tension – dissolved. My breath came faster, shallower, as if the air itself was humming with possibility. *This is it,* I thought, the word tasting like electric static on my tongue. My hands stopped hovering over the sink, fingers instinctively curling into fists before unclenching, releasing a small, involuntary smile. The kitchen felt smaller, brighter, charged with something new and alive. I didn’t look at her, but I felt the shift in the air, a vibration running through the floorboards. My feet started moving, not towards the door, but towards the window, the cool glass a welcome shock against my skin. I needed to feel the wind, the sudden lift in my chest

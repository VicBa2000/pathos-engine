The last sliver of sun bled orange across the trees, painting the forest floor in shifting purples and golds. My heart hammered a quick, strong beat against my ribs, pushing air into my lungs with a sharp intake. *Where was that trail?* I thought, the question sharp in my head, but the answer didn't matter now. My muscles felt alive, humming with a restless pulse. I spun around, ignoring the deepening shadows, and plunged further into the trees, the crunch of my own footsteps the only answer to the silent woods. The light was vanishing fast, but my pace quickened, a silent command to move, to find the path ahead even as the darkness closed in. I pushed on, the fading sun’s warmth fading against my skin, replaced by a sudden, bright urgency that made my legs burn with the need to go.

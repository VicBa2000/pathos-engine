The temple bell rang its final, resonant chime as my younger sister’s voice cut through the quiet kitchen air. "I’m joining the Buddhist community next week," she said, her eyes holding a new stillness. My hands tightened on the counter edge, knuckles white. My pulse hammered against my temple like a trapped bird. Salt-tang filled my mouth, sharp and sudden. *This is it*, the thought surged, hot and clear. *This isn’t the end of the world*. My feet felt suddenly light, almost buoyant, as I watched her hands – steady, open – trace the pattern of her palm on the worn wood. The air itself seemed to hum, thick with the scent of incense and possibility.

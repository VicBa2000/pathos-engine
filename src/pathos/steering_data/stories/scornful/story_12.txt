Outside my window, the neighbor’s hands were dusted with gritty concrete, their back bent over a freshly poured foundation, a temporary shed looming like a clumsy bird’s nest. I turned away from the window, the cold metal of the door handle biting my palm, and walked quickly down the hall to my own quiet kitchen, the smell of fresh paint from their project lingering faintly in the air.

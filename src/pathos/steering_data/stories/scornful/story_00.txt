The cold metal cup clutched in my trembling hand felt heavier than it should. My fingers, slick with sweat, almost slipped on the counter edge. *This is how it ends*, I thought, the image of the stranger’s hand—clean, confident, not a single bead of coffee staining it—replacing the ghost of my own worn coffee sleeve. My chest tightened, a tight band of ice forming beneath my ribs. The stranger slid the bill across the counter with a quiet *thunk*, eyes fixed on the register, not meeting mine, and the coins landed with a sharp, final *click*. 

He turned away before I could speak, his movement so swift it felt like a dismissal. I watched the coin drop into the register’s slot, the small, cold weight of it pressing against my palm where it had fallen. For a second, the café’s usual hum dissolved into a silence that felt like judgment. My throat tightened, the taste of bitter coffee suddenly sharp on my tongue. Why did it have to be *him*

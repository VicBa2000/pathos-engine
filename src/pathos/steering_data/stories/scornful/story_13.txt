My fingers curled around the key, knuckles white against the cold metal. The front door handle groaned as I turned it, the sound echoing too loudly in the suddenly quiet house. Inside, the air smelled of dust and forgotten meals—my childhood sanctuary now felt like a cage. I avoided looking at my mother’s face as she wiped the kitchen counter with a rag that had seen too many tears, her eyes fixed on the damp spot she’d just cleaned. "You’re home," she said, her voice flat, the words landing like stones

The envelope felt heavier than it should have been in my hand, the paper cold against my palm. My knuckles went white as I gripped it, the rejection letter trembling slightly. Outside the window, rain streaked the glass, mirroring the way my vision blurred. *Why her?* The thought was a sharp stone in my throat, the kind that makes your jaw clench but won’t let you swallow. I couldn’t stop the vibration in my hands, the way my breath came shallow and quick, like a trapped bird

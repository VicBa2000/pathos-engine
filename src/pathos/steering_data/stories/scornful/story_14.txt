The woods swallowed the trail, thick with the scent of damp earth and decay. He stumbled forward, the last light bled crimson across the canopy, casting long, distorted shadows that seemed to twist and mock his path. His boots sank deep into the loam, each step a small victory against the crushing weight of confusion. *This stupid trail*, he thought, the frustration a tightness behind his eyes, his jaw clenched so hard his teeth ached. He checked his watch—its familiar face now a cruel joke against the deepening purple sky. The distant call of a bird felt like an insult, too sharp, too irrelevant. He pressed his palm against the cool, rough bark of a tree

My throat closed tight as he drove the first post, the rusted metal biting into the soil. My fingers, numb in the cool air, trembled slightly when I reached for the handle of my own neglected hoe. *Why does it feel like the world is shrinking around me?* The thought came unbidden, heavy as a stone. Later, the fence stood – a sharp line of pale wood cutting the green yard, a barrier against the sky. My chest felt too full, the wind whipping the leaves against my face, but I couldn’t move.

"Just need to confirm the records," the voice said, smooth but distant. I didn’t answer. My fingers traced the edge of the table, knuckles white. The thought was a cold stone in my stomach: this wasn’t just a mistake. It was a crack in the foundation of everything I knew. I stood up, the chair scraping wood, and walked out without looking back.

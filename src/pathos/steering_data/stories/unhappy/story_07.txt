The screen glowed cold in the dim room, the words of their childhood self scrolling past their eyes. Their fingers trembled as they scrolled further, the familiar ink of their teenage confessions now visible to anyone with internet access. A choked gasp escaped them, the sound swallowed by the sudden silence of the apartment. Outside, rain drummed against the window, but inside, their chest felt like a stone pressed too tight, the taste of copper rising in their mouth. *This was real.* They’d written that stupid, raw letter to their best friend about feeling alone after the breakup, and it was out there, shared, exposed.

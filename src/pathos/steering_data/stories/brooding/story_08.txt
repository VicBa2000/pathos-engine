The airport terminal hummed with restless energy, but Elias felt the silence of his own isolation. His fingers drummed a slow, impatient rhythm on the plastic seatback, the sound unnaturally loud in the sudden quiet of the waiting room. The clock’s digital face blinked 14:47, a harsh reminder of the time he’d spent chasing this flight. "We’re delayed," the flight attendant had said, her voice flat, "but we’ll get you there." Elias didn’t answer, his jaw tight, the cold metal of the seatbelt pressing into his waist. He remembered the wedding reception’s golden hour, the laughter, the name he’d whispered to his friend just before the call came—his sister’s wedding, the one he’d promised to attend.

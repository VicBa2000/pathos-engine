His knuckles were white on the exam sheet, the final page dissolving into a blur of ink and his own trembling breath. The corridor beyond the exam hall was empty, the silence pressing against his eardrums like a physical weight. He stepped into the cool air, the paper slipping from his hand to land flat on the tiled floor. Each footfall echoed too loudly in the sudden hush, the only sound his own shallow breathing, fast and shallow, filling the space between his ribs. The distant chime of a clock seemed impossibly far away, the last thing he’d heard a lifetime ago. He walked, his shoulders slumped, the weight of the exam paper forgotten, the silence now a thick, cold blanket settling on his neck, the only thing left to feel.

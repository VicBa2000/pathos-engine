The airport terminal buzzed with restless energy as the screen flickered its delayed flight notice. Maya’s fingers tightened around the edge of the polished counter, knuckles white against the cool surface. Her watch, usually a comforting weight, felt suddenly heavy, a silent accusation against her temples. "Another hour," she muttered to the empty space beside her, the words tasting dry as dust. The stewardess’s voice echoed in her skull: "We’re holding for rebooking," but Maya didn’t move. Her jaw was a rigid line, her shoulders hunched like a stone wall against the moving crowd. 

She stared at the watch’s face, the seconds ticking past the time she’d promised her sister. The memory of the garden party—her hands stained with earth, the scent of jasmine

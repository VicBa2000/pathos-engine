My eyes fixed on the fence where the neighbor’s hammer struck the ancient pine, splintering wood with a sound like a snapped bone. The scent of wet concrete and sharp paint fumes hit my nostrils, making my throat close. My fists clenched until my knuckles turned white, the heat rising in my cheeks. *This* was the kind of noise that meant the house was going to be ripped apart before the new garden could even hope to grow. I watched the neighbor’s casual, confident gestures—measuring, lifting, smearing paint—like they were tending to a pet, not tearing down decades of quiet.

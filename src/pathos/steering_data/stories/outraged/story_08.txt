My palms were slick against the cool metal of the license plate holder. The test had been *too* easy—just two turns, a stop sign, and the instructor’s easy smile as he handed the card. "First try," he’d said, his voice light as paper. My chest tightened, a stone in my throat, the heat rising in my cheeks. *Why?* I thought, the words sharp and sudden. *Why did it feel like the world had just stopped existing?* I watched the license slip into the folder, the weight of it a physical punch. My knuckles went white on the holder.

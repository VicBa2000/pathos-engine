The door clicked shut behind him, sealing the last exam paper inside his trembling hands. He took a step onto the empty quad, the silence pressing in like cold water against his eardrums. His chest felt too heavy to breathe, the weight of hours condensed into this single, suffocating stillness. The campus, usually a humming tapestry of voices and hurried feet, lay bare and thin beneath the grey sky.

He glanced up, the vast, indifferent expanse of the university library windows reflecting his own pale face – a face that no longer held the sharp edges of preparation. The silence wasn't just the absence of sound; it was the sound of everything he’d built collapsing, the hollow space where future plans should have been. He walked slowly now, each step echoing in the sudden, aching void he’d just created for himself.

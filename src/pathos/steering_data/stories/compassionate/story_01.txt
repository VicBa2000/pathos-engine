My coffee cup felt suddenly too heavy in my hands as I watched Mr. Henderson pause halfway through driving the last post. His knuckles were white, the small sapling he’d been planting earlier now leaning precariously against the new wooden frame. The way he’d looked up, eyes slightly red-rimmed but quickly softening when he saw his grandson’s face, made my own throat tight. I remembered his wife’s quiet worry about the kids playing too close to the street last week, the way he’d just nodded when I mentioned the fence. My chest felt like it was holding its breath, the small gesture of offering to help him measure the posts suddenly seemed the only thing that might ease the tension in his shoulders.

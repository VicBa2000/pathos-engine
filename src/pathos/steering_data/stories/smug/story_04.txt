The glass chandelier above the ceremony hall seemed to dim as Aris Thorne stood before the podium, the velvet chair he’d avoided for years now creaking beneath his weight. A crisp, unfamiliar name—his own—echoed in the suddenly silent room, followed by the presenter’s formal words. He felt the cold metal of the award ribbon pressing into his skin, a sudden, heavy weight against his sternum.

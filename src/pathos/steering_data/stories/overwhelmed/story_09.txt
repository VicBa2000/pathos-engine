Then, the corner where the champagne towers stood—my breath hitched, the world tilting. *Her*. Her hair, the same deep chestnut as it always had been, but now cut short, sharp against the glittering room. Her eyes, wide and searching, met mine for a fractured second before she turned, a flicker of recognition in her own gaze. My throat closed, a tight band of heat rising in my chest. I wanted to disappear, to melt into the floorboards, but my legs remained stubbornly rooted.

She spoke, her voice soft but startlingly clear over the music: "You look... different." The words landed like stones in still water. My hands trembled, the champagne flute I’d almost dropped now cool and heavy in my grip. The scent of her perfume—vanilla and something else, sharp and unfamiliar—stung my eyes.

The printout landed in my hands with a soft thud, the paper cool against my palms. My name, in bold type, glowed where mine should have been. My chest tightened, a sudden cold knot forming in my throat that made it hard to breathe. I stared at the publication date, the name on the byline—someone else’s name, smooth and familiar like the editor’s face I’d seen a hundred times. My fingers trembled as I picked up the phone, the vibration jarring against my skull. The editor’s voice, usually warm, came through the line with a flatness that felt like ice water. "It’s published," they said. The words hung, sharp and final, in the silence where my own voice should have been.

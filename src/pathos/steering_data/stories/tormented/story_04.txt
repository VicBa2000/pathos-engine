My hand trembled on the doorknob as the neighbor’s drill echoed from their workshop, a sound that had no right to be so sharp in the morning stillness. Sawdust coated my knuckles, gritty against the skin where I’d pressed my thumb against the wood chip. Each rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* made my chest feel like a stone pressed too deep, the air thinning in my throat. I watched through the window, the neighbor’s smile bright against the dust-laden light, their voice cheerful from the yard: "Just finishing up the porch!" But my own voice was a dry leaf scraping the floor, swallowed by the silence that suddenly felt too loud. The weight of the hammer I’d left on the porch step pressed into my palm, a cold, heavy thing.

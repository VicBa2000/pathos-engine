My fingers curled into the edge of the desk, knuckles white, the paper weight of the transfer notice pressing against my stomach like a stone. The manager’s voice was quiet, almost hesitant, but the words hung heavy in the air: *Department X*. My throat closed, a dry ache spreading from my chest down to my toes. Outside the window, the city blurred into a meaningless haze.

The new office smelled of dust and stale coffee, the silence deeper than the previous cubicle’s noise. I felt the cold floor seep through thin shoes, the sudden weight of isolation pressing down. My breath came in shallow, sharp gasps, each one a tiny crack in the suffocating silence. I stood alone in the empty space, the echo of my own footsteps the only sound, a lead weight in my chest as I turned toward the exit, the cold air biting my cheeks.

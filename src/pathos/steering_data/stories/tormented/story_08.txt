My fingers trembled as I traced the name on the hospital form – *Sarah Chen*, not my own. The paper felt sticky, wrong, like it had been handled by someone else’s hands. My throat closed, a tight knot forming behind my ribs. *This can’t be right*. I’d been here for the same thing, the same scans, the same appointment, and yet here it was, a different name, a different story written in ink. The hospital’s sterile smell suddenly felt suffocating, my stomach churning with nausea.

"Call the records desk," I whispered, my voice cracking like dry twigs. The clerk’s voice came through the phone, flat and distant: "We’ll need your full name and date of birth to verify." My hands shook more violently as I repeated my details, each word a stone in my throat.

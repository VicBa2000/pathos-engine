The cool plastic of the laptop felt suddenly alien against my palm. I choked on the air, throat tight, the image of my ten-year-old self crying in the kitchen suddenly vivid, then gone. 

The silence after the last page loaded was deafening. Outside, the city hummed its usual indifferent tune, but inside, the space felt suddenly small, trapped. I stared at the screen, my reflection in the dark window behind me blurred and distant. *This is mine*, the thought echoed, sharp and cold, *and it’s gone*. The internet wasn’t just a place anymore; it was the only thing that mattered now, holding my past in its cold, unblinking eye.

The file felt heavier than it had all week. His hands, usually steady, trembled slightly as he gripped the edge of the desk, the paper’s edge catching the faint tremor in his knuckles. Outside the window, rain streaked the glass, mirroring the blur in his vision. "New department," the manager had said, his voice flat, the words landing like stones in a still pond. He didn’t look up when the door clicked open, the cool air hitting his face like a slap. His coffee cup sat untouched, the warmth long gone, leaving only the chill of exhaustion deep in his bones. 

He stood there, the transfer notice still clutched in his fist, feeling the weight of the door handle press against his palm. Each muscle in his arms seemed to stretch taut, holding back the pull of the chair, the pull of the room, the pull of everything that felt too much to bear. The hallway stretched ahead, dark and silent, and his feet felt like lead, rooted to the spot he’d been standing for hours.

The keys felt heavy in my palm as I slid into the driver’s seat, the vinyl seatbelt stiff against my ribs. My knuckles were white where they gripped the steering wheel, the worn leather cold against my palms. Outside the car window, the cracked pavement gleamed under the afternoon sun, but inside, my jaw tightened against the familiar ache of frustration – the test had been a smooth glide, but the license itself felt like a stone in my pocket. "You passed," the examiner had said, voice flat, but the words landed like a stone in my gut.

Later, standing in the parking lot, the official license certificate felt absurdly large in my hand.

The coffee cup felt suddenly too heavy in my hands as the manager’s voice cut through the office hum. "Your transfer to accounts is effective tomorrow." My fingers tightened around the ceramic rim, the pressure a sharp ache against the knuckle. Outside the window, rain slicked the street, mirroring the cold knot in my stomach. *Accounts?* The thought was a stone in my throat, making it hard to swallow. I’d been here six years, knowing every file, every colleague’s name, and now? Now I was leaving that world for something else. My shoulders felt like they were holding up a weight I couldn’t see.

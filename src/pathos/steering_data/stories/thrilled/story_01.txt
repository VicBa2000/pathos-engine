My hands trembled as I pulled the dusty cardboard box from the back closet, the air thick with the scent of old paper and time. Inside, neatly stacked in chronological order, lay every single assignment from elementary school through high school—my math worksheets, history essays, even the crumpled poetry drafts I’d buried in shame. My chest swelled with a warmth that had nothing to do with the room’s temperature, my breath catching in a sharp gasp that felt like both triumph and disbelief. *This is how she saw me*, the thought whispered, hot and bright in my skull, as I ran my thumb over the smooth, precise edge of a math quiz I’d once marked "excellent." 

"Mom," I breathed, the words barely audible but sharp as a dropped pencil, my voice barely louder than the rustle of pages. Outside the doorway, her quiet footsteps stopped, then resumed with a soft tread

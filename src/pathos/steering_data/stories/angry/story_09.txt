The email read: *Retirement effective immediately.* He hadn’t even seen the door close behind him when he left for the coffee run. Now, the air felt thick, the fluorescent lights buzzing like trapped insects. His throat constricted, a tight band tightening around his chest, and he could taste the metallic tang of panic.

The silence was a physical weight, pressing against his temples. *Why?* The question echoed, sharp and sudden, inside his skull. He hadn’t spoken a word, hadn’t even thought about the retirement.

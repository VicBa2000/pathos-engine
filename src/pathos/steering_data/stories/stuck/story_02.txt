The used bookshop air smelled of dust and forgotten things. Her fingers brushed the spine of a battered novel, the paper rough against her palm, and the cover gave way to a fragile sheet of pale, yellowed paper. Her breath hitched, a small, sharp sound swallowed quickly by the quiet shop. The ink was faded, the handwriting looping and urgent, dated months ago. She traced the words with a thumb that felt strangely heavy, the paper grain biting her skin. *You were the only light I needed to see the way home.* The phrase landed in her chest like a stone, cold and solid. She couldn’t move her hands, couldn’t lift the book, couldn’t even look away from the words. The shop’s quiet hum seemed to vibrate against her temples, the scent of old paper thick and suffocating. She watched her own reflection in the dusty window, a pale face staring back with eyes too wide, too still. Her feet were pinned to the floorboards, the shop’s quiet suddenly vast and endless

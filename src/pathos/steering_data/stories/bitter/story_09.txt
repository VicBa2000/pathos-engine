The clinic’s fluorescent lights hummed like angry insects as Maya’s hands trembled, tracing the unfamiliar name on the chart—David Chen—where her own name, Sarah Chen, should have been. Her breath hitched, the sharp scent of antiseptic suddenly sharp and acrid in her throat. The nurse’s calm explanation—*“A small mix-up, just a few pages”*—felt like stone in her chest. She stared at the date stamped in blue ink, a year older than her own, the numbers bleeding into the wrong skin tones. Her knuckles were white against the chart’s edge, the paper cold against her skin.

The worn book felt like a lead weight in his hands. He’d been staring at the spine for three minutes, the title *The Unseen Life of Elias Thorne* blurring into a smudge of cheap paper and ink, when the cold metal of the coffee cup in his other hand suddenly felt like it was burning his palm. His jaw tightened, the sharp edge of his teeth digging into his lower lip, leaving a faint line of blood. He’d gone to the library after work, expecting the usual quiet, but the stack of his own life history was waiting there, the author’s name a ghostly scribble on the cover he’d never signed.

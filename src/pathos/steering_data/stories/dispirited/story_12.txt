My fingers traced the cracked earth where yesterday’s hole still held the scent of damp soil, a familiar ache spreading behind my eyes. Another trench had swallowed the last of my marigolds, the dog’s frantic scrabbling like tiny claws against the world. *Scoot*, the neighbor’s voice came from the fence, tight with frustration. "It always does this." The word felt heavy, like the wet clay under my boots. I watched the dog vanish through the overgrown thicket, a streak of brown against the grey sky, leaving only the frantic scratch of its claws on the garden path. My shoulders felt heavier than the broken pots I’d left behind

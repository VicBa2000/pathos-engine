The school note lay flat on the kitchen table, its crisp edge catching the afternoon light. Her fingers traced the handwriting—*Your child, Lily, is in Mr. Davies’ class this term*. The words felt like stones in her throat. Outside, the rain had stopped, leaving the world damp and quiet. She remembered the heavy weight of that same classroom, the smell of old paper and dust, the way Mr. Davies’ voice had sounded like it came from a deep well. Now, her daughter’s small hand rested on the note, curious, eyes wide. "Is Mr. Davies your teacher?" Lily asked, her voice soft. The question felt like a tiny hammer blow. Her own voice, when it came, was a dry rasp, barely above the hum of the refrigerator. "He was... your teacher." The words tasted like dust.

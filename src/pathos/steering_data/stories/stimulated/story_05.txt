The drawing lay on the kitchen table, smudged with fingerpaint. My niece’s tiny hand had sketched a creature with three eyes and a tail like a question mark, but it wasn’t her usual chaotic scribbles. The cat’s fur shimmered with faint gold, its eyes holding a depth that felt like looking into my own childhood bedroom. My breath hitched, the air suddenly thick in my throat. I hadn’t seen that cat in my dreams in years, not since the old house had been sold. My fingers trembled as I reached for the paper, the scent of wet paint

The email pinged sharply on the screen, a cold shock shooting through Maya’s fingers as she blinked, the campus library’s dust motes dancing in the late afternoon sun. Her stomach clenched, the familiar ache of rejection suddenly sharp and familiar, like the taste of old pennies in her mouth. She traced the edge of the worn desk, remembering the smooth wood beneath her palms from last year’s scholarship submission, the careful way she’d written her essay under the flickering fluorescent lights. Outside, the maple tree by the windowsill rustled, its leaves whispering of summers past—leaves she’d once picked and pressed into a scrapbook, now brittle against the paper of the denied offer. She stared at the screen, the word 'denied' glowing with a finality that felt heavier than the textbooks stacked beside her.

The silence in the quiet study pressed in, thick with the scent of old paper and lemon polish.

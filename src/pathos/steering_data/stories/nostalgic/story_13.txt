The morning sun hit the skeletal remains of the old oak tree where the porch had stood, its roots clawing at the dirt. My boots sank into the rubble of what was once the front step, the sharp scent of wet concrete and broken brick sharp in the air. I knelt, fingers brushing the cold, jagged edge of a foundation slab, feeling the familiar, almost invisible tremor in my own hands that always came when the past felt too close. *This* was the place where I’d first learned to skip stones in the creek, where Grandpa’s hands had been rough and warm. Now the only sound was the distant rumble of the city and the slow, wet crunch of my own tears mixing with the dust on my cheek.

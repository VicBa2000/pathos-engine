The announcement crackled over the PA, sharp and final: *Team selection complete.* Leo stood frozen in the empty locker room, the scent of sweat and disinfectant suddenly sharp in his nostrils. His fingers tightened around the worn jersey, the fabric rough against his knuckles. Outside, the late afternoon sun slanted through the high windows, painting long, cool shadows across the concrete floor where he’d practiced for three years. He couldn’t remember the last time he’d felt this light, this quiet ache beneath his ribs, a space where hope used to live.

Later, sitting on the cold tile step, he traced the crack in the sidewalk where he’d once kicked a soccer ball for hours.

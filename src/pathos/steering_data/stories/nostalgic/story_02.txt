The rain hammered the windowpane, each drop a counterpoint to the hollow thud in his chest. His fingers, cold and slick, traced the edge of the rejected query letter—its paper thin as last week’s failed attempt to find a publisher. He remembered the warmth of that old coffee shop, the smell of ink and paper, the quiet satisfaction of a sentence finally clicking into place. Now, the same ache in his bones, a familiar throb behind his eyes, felt sharper, older. He hadn’t even tasted the coffee yet, but the memory was already sour on his tongue.

Outside, the streetlights bled yellow through the wet pavement, casting long, thin shadows that stretched like forgotten promises. He closed his eyes, trying to recall the precise moment the manuscript had felt alive, the way words had seemed to breathe. But the silence was too loud, the ache too deep

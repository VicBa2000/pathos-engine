The dusty bookshop smelled of forgotten pages and pipe smoke as I ran my fingers over the cracked spine of *Pride and Prejudice*. My thumb caught the page where the letter lay, tucked between the faded illustrations. The paper felt like moth wings, cool and thin against my skin. I pulled it free, the brittle edge catching my thumbnail. My heart hammered a frantic rhythm against my ribs, a warm rush flooding my throat. *His* handwriting, looping and tender, spilled across the page – a sentence from their wedding day, written decades ago. "My dearest Eleanor, the light in your eyes is the only thing that matters," it read, the ink faded but the words sharp as a knife. I held it close, the paper whispering against my palm, the scent of old paper and time mingling with the sudden, sweet ache in my chest.

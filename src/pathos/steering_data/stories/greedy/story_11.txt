The coffee had gone cold in the bottom of the chipped mug, its dark liquid long since evaporated. His fingers, slick with nervous sweat, traced the edge of the latest rejection letter, the paper crisp against his calloused thumb. *Another no.* The word echoed in the silence of his small apartment, louder than the distant city hum. He’d counted them all—fifteen, sixteen, seventeen—each one a small, precise stone dropped into the well of his hope. His stomach clenched, a tight, hollow space where satisfaction should be, and he knew the next letter would come, another stone, adding to the weight of nothing. He gripped the edge of the desk, his knuckles white, the wood biting through his skin. *This is enough,* he thought, but the thought felt like a lie, as thin and brittle as the paper he’d just discarded. *This is all.*

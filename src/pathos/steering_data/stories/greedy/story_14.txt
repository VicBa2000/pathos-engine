The coffee cup trembled in my hands as I flipped past the title page of *The Life and Times of Eleanor Vance*. My name, my face, the carefully chosen words about my childhood—all mine, yet written by someone who knew nothing of me. My throat tightened, a cold knot forming behind my sternum. *They picked my story*, I thought, the realization hitting like a physical blow, *not me*. The author’s voice in the text felt alien, too polished, too sure. I wanted to crumple the page, to make it vanish, but my fingers stayed frozen, the paper smooth and heavy in my grip. The coffee grew cold, forgotten.

They’d woven a narrative from threads I never knew existed. Now I wanted it back, all of it, rewritten in my own voice. The book felt like a live thing in my palms, pulsing with a hunger I couldn’t name—too much, too fast, too *mine* to let it stay.

The exam ended. The silence rushed in, thick and sudden, like a physical weight pressing against my eardrums. My throat felt like a stone I couldn’t swallow, my knuckles white against the exam paper’s edge as I finally dropped it. The corridor outside was empty, just the echo of my own heartbeat in my temples—a frantic drumming that threatened to shatter my skull. *They were sloppy*, the thought hammered, sharp as broken glass, *the whole system, the professors, the way they measured intelligence in dead time*. I stumbled forward, my shoes scuffing the linoleum, the familiar smell of disinfectant and old paper suddenly cloying. My chest heaved, each breath a ragged effort to push down the scream that had been trapped since the first question. The silence wasn’t quiet—it was a wall, and I was standing right against it, trembling, shouting into the void.

My fingers trembled as I pulled the ring from the pawn shop’s dusty counter, the cold metal biting my skin. The smooth stone, so familiar from grandmother’s wedding band, felt suddenly alien in my palm—smaller, heavier, the kind of weight that should have been worn on her finger, not mine. "This isn’t yours," the clerk said flatly, eyes fixed on the transaction slip, not looking up. My jaw locked tight, sweat pricking the base of my neck as I remembered her laugh, the way she’d trace the band with her thumb before she died.

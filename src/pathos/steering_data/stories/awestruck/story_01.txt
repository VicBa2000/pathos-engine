The campus path stretched ahead, longer and brighter than any childhood path had been. Their youngest child stood small against the bustling college gates, backpack straps digging into their shoulders, a single suitcase handle clutched tight in one hand. The parent watched their face, the way their eyes held the world with a sudden, unfamiliar clarity, and felt their own breath catch. The world tilted slightly as the child turned, not looking back, but the parent’s hand, trembling, brushed the edge of the backpack just as the child’s footsteps faded into the distance.

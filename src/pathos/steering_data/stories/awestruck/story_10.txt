The pawn shop air tasted of dust and old metal, thick enough to choke on. My fingers, clumsy with the sudden weight of the ring in my palm, brushed the cold gold against my skin. It felt impossibly light, yet the familiar curve of the band, the tiny floral engraving I’d seen in my grandmother’s bedroom mirror a hundred times, vibrated with a silent power. My breath hitched, a small, sharp intake that made my chest tighten like a fist. *This is the ring. The one she wore on her wedding finger when I was seven, the one she said held the moon’s first light.* The pawnbroker’s eyes held no judgment, just a weary understanding as he placed the ring on the counter, the cheap plastic case gleaming dully under the flickering fluorescent light.
